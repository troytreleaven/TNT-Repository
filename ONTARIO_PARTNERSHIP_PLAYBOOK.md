# Partnership & Channel Strategy Playbook
## Ontario Construction Market Entry through Industry Associations

**Prepared for:** Gianluca / DesignCore Training  
**Date:** February 2026  
**Objective:** Establish credibility and market access through strategic partnerships with Ontario construction industry associations

---

## Executive Summary

This playbook provides a tactical roadmap for entering the Ontario ICI (Industrial, Commercial, Institutional) construction market through strategic partnerships with industry associations. The strategy leverages a critical market gap: associations have trust and access but limited live delivery capability, while DesignCore Training offers premium, instructor-led programs that can often be 100% funded through the Canada-Ontario Job Grant.

**Key Strategic Advantage:** Position training as "FREE to eligible employers" through COJG funding, removing price objections while maintaining premium positioning.

## 2. PARTNERSHIP MODELS

### Model A: Co-Branded Programs

**Structure:**
- Joint branding: "[Association Name] - DesignCore [Program Name]"
- Association provides: Member access, promotion, venue (if applicable)
- DesignCore provides: Curriculum, instructor, materials, administration

**Revenue Share Options:**
- Option 1: 70/30 split (DC/Association) on net revenue
- Option 2: Fixed license fee per program ($5,000-15,000)
- Option 3: Revenue share on non-member registrations only

**Example Programs:**
- "OGCA Project Leadership Intensive"
- "ORBA Young Professionals Academy"
- "TCA Construction Management Excellence Series"

**Contract Terms to Negotiate:**
- Minimum 2-year exclusivity for specific program categories
- Association commitment to promote to member base (minimum 2 email sends)
- Right of first refusal on new program development
- Use of association logo and member testimonials

---

### Model B: Exclusive Provider Agreements

**Structure:**
- DesignCore becomes the association's exclusive provider for specific training categories
- Association endorses DC as preferred training partner
- Members get preferential pricing (15-25% discount)

**Categories to Target:**
- Advanced project management
- Leadership development
- Construction technology/BIM
- Executive education

**Value Exchange:**
- Association gets: Revenue share, enhanced member value, no delivery overhead
- DC gets: Exclusive access to member base, credibility boost, predictable lead flow

**Contract Terms:**
- 12-24 month initial term
- Performance milestones (minimum enrollments)
- Annual review and renewal
- Exclusivity limited to specific geographies/program types

---

**Structure:**
- One-off or quarterly seminars/workshops

**Structure:**

## 4. CANADA-ONTARIO JOB GRANT STRATEGY

### Understanding COJG

**Program Basics:**
- Federal-provincial partnership
- Funds training for existing and new employees
- Up to $10,000 per trainee (general)
- **Up to $15,000 per trainee for small employers hiring unemployed individuals**

**Employer Size Definitions:**
- **Small Employer:** Less than 100 employees
- **Large Employer:** 100+ employees

**Funding Levels:**
| Employer Size | Hiring Status | Government Share | Employer Share | Max per Trainee |
|--------------|---------------|------------------|----------------|-----------------|
| Small (<100) | Unemployed | 100% | $0 | $15,000 |
| Small (<100) | Existing | 5/6 (83%) | 1/6 (17%) | $10,000 |
| Large (100+) | Any | 50% | 50% | $10,000 |

**Eligible Training:**
- Must be delivered by third-party trainer
- Must fill a skills gap
- Must lead to job advancement/retention
- Cannot be part of mandatory certification

---

### Positioning Programs as COJG-Eligible

**Key Messaging:**
"Most DesignCore training programs are eligible for Canada-Ontario Job 
Grant funding. For small employers, this can mean up to 100% of training

**Eligibility Criteria DC Programs Meet:**
✓ Delivered by third-party training provider (DesignCore)
✓ Fills identified skills gap (project management, leadership)
✓ Leads to better job performance
✓ Not mandatory certification
✓ Measurable outcomes

**Program Categories to Emphasize:**
1. Project Management Excellence
2. Construction Leadership Development
3. Estimating and Bid Preparation
4. Construction Technology/BIM
5. Business Development for Contractors
6. Field Operations Management

---

### Application Process Overview

**Step-by-Step Process:**

**Step 1: Eligibility Check**
- Confirm employer size (<100 employees for maximum benefit)
- Confirm trainee employment status
- Identify skills gap

**Step 2: Training Plan Development**
- Define learning objectives
- Outline curriculum
- Specify duration and delivery method
- Estimate costs

**Step 3: Application Completion**
- Complete COJG application form
- Attach supporting documents
- Submit to Service Provider

**Step 4: Approval Wait**
- Typically 4-6 weeks
- May require additional information

**Step 5: Training Delivery**
- Must occur AFTER approval
- Must be completed within timeframe specified

**Step 6: Reimbursement**
- Submit proof of completion
- Submit receipts/invoices
- Receive grant payment
