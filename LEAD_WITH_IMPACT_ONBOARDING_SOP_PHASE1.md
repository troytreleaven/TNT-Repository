## 1. Purpose & Overview

### Purpose
This SOP trains onboarding coaches to conduct **Lead with Impact onboarding calls** that prepare participants for their 3-day Leadership Boot Camp. This process bridges enrollment and training delivery, ensuring participants are mentally prepared and trainers have complete information.

### Call Duration
- **Standard calls:** 60 minutes
- **Hutton group calls:** 30 minutes (streamlined version)

### The Transformation Formula
**EC + BC = PC**  
(Emotional Change + Behavior Change = Performance Change)

This is what makes Lead with Impact unique - we address emotional, behavioral, AND performance change together.

## 4. Pre-Call Preparation (15 minutes before)

### Step 1: Access Documents
**Salesforce users:**
- Contact → Related → Files

**Non-Salesforce users:**
- Jen sends documents separately

### Step 2: Reading Order & Time

1. **PIF (2 min)** - Job title, industry, location, manager info, goals
2. **MIF (2 min)** - Manager's feedback, expected outcomes  
3. **DISC (1 min)** - Note their style (C/D/I/S)
4. **Onboarding Summary (2 min)** - Executive overview
5. **Onboarding Report (5 min)** - This is what you'll share with them

**Total prep time: ~12-15 minutes**

### What to Look For:
- **Goals:** How would more confidence help them lead?
- **Strengths/Challenges:** What they mentioned
- **Industry context:** Construction, professional services, etc.
- **How they're measured:** Success metrics
- **Their "why":** Future aspirations
- **Manager relationship:** Who's involved

## 5. The Onboarding Call Flow

### Opening (2 minutes)

**Script:**
> *"Hi [Name], I'm [Your Name], your onboarding coach. I'm not the trainer, but I'm here to help you get ready for the training by walking through your personalized plan. I want to share it with you and get your thoughts. If there's anything we should let the trainer know, we can do that today as well."*

### Part 1: DISC Style Analysis (5-7 minutes)

**Start here:** Go to the DISC Style Analysis section in the Onboarding Report.

**Script:**
> *"I'm going to read the first sentence about your style. Just tell me if it resonates with you."*

Read their style summary aloud.

**Ask:** *"Does that sit right with you?"*

**Then review:**
- Personal summary points
- Things to watch out for
- Their specific blend (e.g., "You've adopted a C/D style, but your natural core is I/S")

**DISC Adaptation - Describe the course THEIR way:**

- **Conscientious (C):** *"You'll love the processes. We teach three specific communication formulas - the Magic Formula, the Lion's Formula, and a way to disagree agreeably."*

- **Influencer (I):** *"You'll love the people. Everybody's going to be having a good time. It's fun, lots of conversation and practice."*

- **Dominant (D):** *"You'll get quick results. You'll notice changes immediately. You'll get direct attention and you'll like the pace - it's going to go really quickly."*

- **Steady (S):** *"You'll love the environment. The people you're with are going to be important, and this is something you can really trust. It's proven over 113 years to really work."*

### Part 2: The 5 Drivers (3-5 minutes)

**Script:**
> *"The boot camp you're going to covers five key areas:
> 1. Confidence
> 2. Communication (speaking and listening)
> 3. Human Relations (relating to people, getting cooperation)
> 4. Leadership (taking people places they wouldn't go alone)
> 5. Attitude Control (managing stress, living with enthusiasm)
> 
> Through those five areas, you're going to reach your goals."*

**Ask:** *"Of those five, which one stands out as most useful for you right now, based on what we've been talking about?"*

**Follow-up:** *"How would that look if you had more [their choice]?"*

### Part 3: Vision Statement (2-3 minutes)

**Script:**
> *"I'm going to read your vision statement. I know you can read, but I want you to hear it from outside yourself."*

Read their vision aloud.

**Ask:** *"How does that resonate with you?"*

**Say:** *"On Day 1 of your boot camp, you'll create your own version of this. We wanted to show you what it could look like."*

### Part 4: Outcome-Based Job Description (5-10 minutes)

**For 60-minute calls only:**

**Opening:**
> *"The rest of this document is a practical plan. There are two tools: an Outcome-Based Job Description and a Monthly Project List."*

**The Parent Analogy for Key Result Areas:**
> *"Think about being a parent. You have to feed your kids, clothe them, house them, love them, educate them. If you only do one of those five, you'll have a troubled child, right? Same with your job - you need to do all your Key Result Areas to be effective. Some you do daily (like feeding kids), some less often."*

**Performance Standards:**
> *"This is how you'll be measured - the outcome, not just the activity."*

**Monthly Project List:**
> *"This is your scorecard. Set specific goals under each Key Result Area for the month. Make them SMART - specific, measurable."*

**For 30-minute calls (Hutton):** Briefly mention these documents exist but don't review in detail.

## 6. Handling Common Questions

**"What should I bring?"**
> *"Bring something to write with and a pad of paper. You won't need your laptop or phone during the course - we'll show you the platform but won't use it during training."*

**"What should I wear?"**
> [Answer based on venue knowledge]

**"Do I have to give speeches?"**
> *"We're not going to have you make fancy speeches. You'll talk about how you're using the principles, but it's not a formal presentation. It's conversational, not oratory."*

**"When is it?"**
> [Provide logistics]

**"Can I see my DISC results?"**
> *"We have your DISC assessment available at no extra charge. Would you like it? If you wouldn't use it, just decline - it's okay."*

**"What careers suit my DISC style?"**
> *"Generally: C's gravitate to technical/accounting, I's to sales/customer service, D's to management, S's to support roles. But people succeed in all styles!"*

## 7. Key Coaching Principles

1. **Start with Praise** - Always begin with something positive from their assessment
2. **Anchor in the Future** - Connect everything to their stated goals ("I know you want to lead a team...")
3. **Ask, Don't Tell** - "Does that resonate?" "How would that look?"
4. **Emotional First, Technical Second** - Start with personality/discussion, then job description mechanics
5. **The Transformation Formula** - Mention: *"EC + BC = PC"* (Emotional + Behavior = Performance)

## 8. Quality Checklist

### Before Call:
- [ ] Read PIF, MIF, DISC, Summary, Report (15 min)
- [ ] Note their industry and goals
- [ ] Check their DISC style
- [ ] Have documents ready to screen share
- [ ] Two screens recommended (or split screen)

### During Call:
- [ ] Started with DISC style analysis
- [ ] Asked if it resonated
- [ ] Covered 5 drivers
- [ ] Asked which driver matters most
- [ ] Read vision statement
- [ ] Answered logistics questions
- [ ] Set expectations for boot camp

### After Call:
- [ ] Notes added to Salesforce/OCR
- [ ] Trainer notified of special considerations
- [ ] Documents saved to participant file

## 9. Special Notes

### For Hutton Group (30-min calls):
- Skip detailed job description review
- Focus on DISC, 5 drivers, and vision
- They're not doing labs - just 3-day boot camp
- Group onboarding (3-4 people) - streamline

### For Standard Onboarding (60-min calls):
- Full job description review with parent analogy
- Detailed monthly project list discussion
- More time for questions

### If Documents Don't Resonate:
> *"You know what, every now and then our team gets busy and there might be an error. Let me apologize and we'll check our notes and get back to you."*

### Practice Recommendation:
- Practice with each other first
- Record practice sessions
- Review with Troy if needed
- Add new insights/questions to this SOP as you learn
