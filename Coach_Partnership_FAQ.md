# COACH PARTNERSHIP FAQ
## Dale Carnegie Business Group & Independent Coaches

---

## 🤝 PARTNERSHIP PHILOSOPHY

### Why This Partnership Model?

Dale Carnegie Business Group (DC) believes in **partnership, not control**. We work with top-tier independent coaches because:
- We respect your expertise and independent status
- We provide brand, curriculum, and client access
- Together, we deliver exceptional value to clients

This FAQ explains how we protect our business interests while respecting yours.

---

## 📋 GENERAL QUESTIONS

### Q1: Am I becoming a Dale Carnegie employee?

**No.** You remain an independent contractor with your own business. This means:
- You control how, when, and where you work
- You're responsible for your own taxes (HST/GST, income tax)
- You maintain your own business insurance
- You can work with other clients and training firms
- You're not entitled to employee benefits (vacation pay, EI, CPP from DC)

**Why this matters**: It preserves your independence and prevents employment misclassification issues.

---

### Q2: Can I work with other training companies while working with DC?

**Yes.** This is a non-exclusive partnership. You can work with:
- Other training/consulting firms
- Your own private coaching clients
- Corporate clients you source independently

**The only restriction**: You cannot use OMTA-specific information obtained through this engagement to solicit OMTA for competing services during and for 12 months after the engagement.

---

### Q3: What does "competing services" mean?

Competing services are training, coaching, or leadership development services that would substitute for or compete with Dale Carnegie's offerings to OMTA.

**Examples of competing services**:
- Leadership coaching for OMTA
- Team development workshops for OMTA
- DISC assessments for OMTA
- Communication skills training for OMTA

**Examples of non-competing services**:
- Executive coaching in areas outside Dale Carnegie's core curriculum
- Consulting on operational or technical matters
- Services for organizations unrelated to OMTA

---

### Q4: What if I already have a relationship with OMTA?

**Disclose it upfront.** Before signing the agreement, you'll complete a Relationship Disclosure Schedule listing:
- Any pre-existing business relationships with OMTA
- Personal or family connections to OMTA decision-makers
- Any other potential conflicts of interest

**Why this matters**: Transparency protects everyone. If you already know OMTA personnel, that's fine—we just need to know about it.

## 💰 COMPENSATION & BILLING

### Q5: How and when do I get paid?

- You invoice DC within 5 business days of service delivery
- DC pays within 30 days of invoice receipt
- Late payments accrue interest at 1.5% per month
- You receive a T4A (Canada) or 1099 (US) for tax reporting

**Important**: You're responsible for your own income tax, HST/GST, and other statutory obligations.

---

### Q6: Why can't I bill OMTA directly?

DC maintains the master contractual relationship with OMTA. This means:
- DC holds the contract and bears the commercial risk
- DC manages renewals, expansions, and client satisfaction
- All billing flows through DC for simplicity and legal clarity

This is standard for contractor relationships and protects both parties.

---

### Q7: What expenses are covered?

Pre-approved expenses are reimbursed at cost:
- Travel to/from OMTA locations
- Materials and supplies specifically for the engagement
- Other reasonable business expenses (with prior approval)

Keep receipts and submit with your invoice.

## 🛡️ CONFIDENTIALITY & IP

### Q8: What information is considered confidential?

**Confidential Information includes**:
- OMTA's business plans and organizational data not publicly available
- OMTA personnel contact information you obtain through this engagement
- DC's proprietary curriculum materials (marked as such)
- Fee structures and commercial arrangements between DC and OMTA

**Not confidential**:
- Publicly available information
- Information you already knew before the engagement
- Information you independently develop without using confidential info

---

### Q9: How long do confidentiality obligations last?

- **General confidential information**: 3 years from disclosure
- **Contact lists and trade secrets**: Indefinite

This is standard for professional services agreements.

---

### Q10: Who owns materials I create?

**It depends**:

**DC owns**:
- Materials created specifically for this engagement (customized exercises, assessments)
- Adaptations of Dale Carnegie curriculum
- Work product delivered to OMTA

**You keep**:
- Your pre-existing proprietary methods
- Published books, articles, or frameworks
- General coaching tools not developed for this engagement
- Your moral rights (attribution) to your contributions

---

### Q11: Can I use my own coaching exercises and tools?

**Yes**, with caveats:
- You can use your own general coaching frameworks
- You cannot use tools that directly compete with Dale Carnegie proprietary content
- If you develop new tools specifically for this engagement, DC owns them

**Best practice**: Discuss any proprietary tools you want to use before the engagement begins.

## ⚠️ RESTRICTIONS & NON-SOLICITATION

### Q12: What can't I do during and after the engagement?

**During the engagement and for 12 months after**:
- Solicit OMTA directly for services that compete with Dale Carnegie offerings
- Use OMTA contact information obtained through this engagement to market competing services
- Circumvent DC to contract directly with OMTA for Dale Carnegie-type services

**You CAN**:
- Continue existing business relationships
- Do general marketing (LinkedIn, newsletters, public speaking)
- Accept non-competing consulting opportunities
- Work with other clients and training firms

---

### Q13: What if OMTA approaches me directly?

If OMTA personnel contact you directly for Dale Carnegie-related services:
1. Thank them for their interest
2. Refer them back to DC within 48 hours
3. Let DC manage the conversation and contract

**Why**: DC maintains the client relationship and manages commercial terms.

---

### Q14: What happens if I violate the non-solicitation clause?

Violations can result in:
- Immediate termination of the agreement
- Legal action to enforce the agreement
- Potential damages for breach of contract

**The good news**: The restrictions are narrow and reasonable. Most coaches have no issue complying.

## 📝 INSURANCE & LIABILITY

### Q15: Why do I need insurance?

Professional liability insurance (Errors & Omissions) protects:
- You, if a client claims your services caused harm
- DC, if a client sues both parties
- Everyone, by ensuring there's coverage for claims

**Requirements**:
- Professional Liability: $2M minimum
- General Liability: $1M minimum
- DC named as additional insured

---

### Q16: What if something goes wrong during a session?

**If there's an incident**:
1. Notify DC immediately
2. Document what happened
3. Cooperate with any investigation

**Liability framework**:
- You indemnify DC for claims arising from your negligence or misconduct
- DC indemnifies you for claims arising from DC's negligence
- Each party's liability is capped at fees paid in the preceding 12 months

**Bottom line**: Carry insurance, act professionally, and communicate openly.

## 🔚 TERMINATION & POST-ENGAGEMENT

### Q17: How can the agreement be ended?

**Either party can terminate**:
- With 14 days' written notice (no cause needed)
- Immediately for material breach (with 7-day cure period)
- Immediately for gross misconduct or ethical violations

---

### Q18: What happens after termination?

**You must**:
- Return all DC materials and confidential information
- Continue honoring confidentiality obligations
- Comply with non-solicitation restrictions for 12 months

**DC must**:
- Pay all outstanding fees for services rendered
- Provide any agreed-upon transition assistance

---

### Q19: Can I re-engage with OMTA after the 12-month period?

**Yes.** After the 12-month non-solicitation period expires, you can:
- Work with OMTA directly (if they approach you)
- Market to OMTA (if not using DC-obtained contact info)
- Provide non-competing services

**Best practice**: Notify DC of any post-restriction work with OMTA as a courtesy.
