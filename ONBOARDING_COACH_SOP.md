## 1. Overview

### Purpose
The onboarding call establishes rapport with participants, introduces the Lead with Impact program framework, and ensures participants are prepared for their training journey.

### Call Duration
- **Standard:** 60 minutes
- **Hutton Group:** 30 minutes (abbreviated)

### Your Role
You are the **Onboarding Coach** — not the trainer. Your job is to:
- Review the Onboarding Report with the participant
- Gather feedback for the trainer
- Build excitement and confidence

## 2. Pre-Call Preparation

**Time Required:** 15 minutes before the call

### Documents to Gather

| Document | Location | Purpose |
|----------|----------|---------|
| **PIF** (Personal Info Sheet) | Salesforce → Contact → Related → Files | Job title, industry, goals |
| **MIF** (Manager Info Sheet) | Salesforce → Contact → Related → Files | Manager's perspective |
| **DISC Assessment** | Salesforce → Contact → Related → Files | Personality style analysis |
| **Coaching Report** | Salesforce → Contact → Related → Files | High-level summary |
| **Onboarding Report** | Salesforce → Contact → Related → Files | Participant-facing document |

### Pre-Call Review Order

1. **PIF** - Scan for job title, industry, stated development goals
2. **MIF** - Review manager's perspective on strengths, challenges, career path
3. **DISC** - Note adopted vs. natural style (e.g., "C/D adopted from I/S")
4. **Coaching Report** - Read "Coaching Opportunities" section
5. **Onboarding Report** - This is the ONLY document shared with the participant

### Preparation Checklist

- [ ] All documents downloaded and reviewed
- [ ] Key talking points identified from Coaching Report
- [ ] DISC style noted for insightful questions
- [ ] Participant's goals memorized
- [ ] Manager's concerns noted

## 3. Onboarding Call Structure

### Phase 1: Introduction (5 mins)

**Script:**
> "Hi [Name], I'm [Your Name], your onboarding coach. I'm here to review your Onboarding Report and answer any questions before your training begins. This call is about setting you up for success. Let's get started!"

**Key Points:**
- Position yourself as the onboarding coach (not the trainer)
- Set expectation: this is a review and preparation call
- Ask: "Do you have your Onboarding Report in front of you?"

---

### Phase 2: Personal Summary & DISC (15 mins)

**Purpose:** Build rapport by starting with the participant's personality and goals

**Script:**
> "Let's start with your personal summary. I'm going to read this aloud — does this resonate with you?"

**Actions:**
1. Share your screen (or have them open their Onboarding Report)
2. Read the "DISC Style Analysis Summary" aloud
3. Ask: "Does this feel accurate to you?"
4. Discuss "Things to watch out for" — get their perspective

**Key Questions:**
- "How does your manager see your style?"
- "Where do you see this showing up in your work?"

---

### Phase 3: Program Framework (15 mins)

**Purpose:** Connect the program's value to the participant's goals

**The 5 Dale Carnegie Drivers:**

| Driver | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Confidence** | Building self-assurance and presence |
| **Communication** | Effective interpersonal skills |
| **Human Relations** | Building stronger relationships |
| **Leadership** | Influencing others positively |
| **Attitude Control** | Maintaining positive mindset |

**The Leadership Transformation Formula:**

```
Emotional Change → Behavior Change → Performance Change
```

**Script:**
> "The Lead with Impact program is built around 5 key drivers. Which of these stands out as most impactful for you right now?"

**Actions:**
1. Introduce each driver briefly
2. Ask which resonates most
3. Read the "Vision" statement from their report
4. Explain they'll create their own version on Day 1

---

### Phase 4: Job Description & Scorecard (15 mins)

**Purpose:** Frame the job description as an outcome-based "North Star"

**Key Concepts:**

- **Outcome-Based Job Description** - Focuses on *outcomes* (Performance Standards) and *why* they do the job (Commitment), not just duties

- **KRAs (Key Result Areas)** - Essential responsibilities (like "feeding, clothing, housing" — all must be done for success)

- **Monthly Project List** - A scorecard template they can use or adapt

**Script:**
> "Your job description isn't a list of tasks — it's your North Star. Let's look at your Outcome-Based Job Description..."

**Actions:**
1. Review key outcomes and commitments
2. Explain how this differs from traditional job descriptions
3. Introduce the Monthly Project List as a tool

---

### Phase 5: Logistics & Q&A (10 mins)

**Common Questions to Address:**

| Question | Response |
|----------|----------|
| "What should I bring?" | Notebook, pen, open mind |
| "What should I wear?" | Business casual — be comfortable but professional |
| "Will I have to give speeches?" | No formal speeches — practical application and discussions |
| "What if I'm nervous?" | Totally normal! Most participants feel the same |

**Closing Script:**
> "You're going to do great. The program is practical, not academic — you'll be applying concepts right away. Any final questions before we wrap up?"

## 4. Hutton Group Exceptions

The Hutton group has a **30-minute abbreviated call**:

| Standard Call | Hutton Group Call |
|--------------|------------------|
| 60 minutes | 30 minutes |
| Full job description review | Skip — job descriptions already defined |
| All 5 drivers + full framework | Focus on personal summary + 5 drivers |

**Hutton Group Script Adjustment:**
> "For Hutton group participants, your manager has already defined your job description, so we'll focus on your personal summary and the 5 Dale Carnegie drivers."

## 6. Common Scenarios & FAQs

### "My manager didn't fill out the MIF"

**Response:** "No problem — we'll focus on what we do have. Let's look at your DISC profile and personal summary instead."

---

### "I don't understand my DISC style"

**Response:** "That's totally normal! DISC can be nuanced. Let me explain the four styles: Conscientious (analytical), Influencer (persuasive), Dominant (decisive), Steady (supportive). Where do you see yourself?"

---

### "I'm nervous about the course"

**Response:** "That's completely normal! Most participants feel the same way initially. The program is very practical — it's about applying concepts, not giving formal presentations."

---

### "Can I skip the job description part?"

**Response:** "I'd recommend reviewing it — it's designed to help you focus on outcomes rather than just tasks. Even a quick review can be valuable."

---

### "When do I get my DISC report?"

**Response:** "Great question — I can check on that for you after our call. Would you like me to confirm the process?"
