# COACH PARTNERSHIP AGREEMENT
## Executive Summary for Initial Discussions

---

### 🎯 PURPOSE
This agreement establishes a professional partnership between **Dale Carnegie Business Group (DC)** and **Independent Coaches** for delivering training services to the Ontario Massage Therapist Association (OMTA).

---

### 🤝 THE PARTNERSHIP MODEL

**What DC Provides:**
- Client relationships and introductions
- Dale Carnegie brand, curriculum, and methodology
- Administrative support (scheduling, invoicing)
- Professional liability coverage for DC's portion

**What You Provide:**
- Professional coaching delivery
- Your expertise and independent contractor status
- Professional liability insurance ($2M minimum)
- Adherence to Dale Carnegie values and standards

---

### 📋 KEY TERMS AT A GLANCE

| Element | Terms |
|---------|-------|
| **Status** | Independent Contractor (not employee) |
| **Exclusivity** | None - you can work with other firms |
| **Compensation** | Agreed hourly/per diem rate, paid within 30 days |
| **Billing** | DC bills client; you invoice DC |
| **Term** | Project-based or defined term with 14-day termination notice |

---

### 🛡️ PROTECTIONS & OBLIGATIONS

#### What We Protect:
- **Client Relationships**: DC maintains the OMTA relationship
- **Confidentiality**: OMTA information and DC proprietary materials (3-year term)
- **Intellectual Property**: DC owns work product created for the engagement
- **Brand Integrity**: Services delivered consistent with Dale Carnegie standards

#### What You Keep:
- Your pre-existing proprietary methods and published works
- Your independent business status
- Ability to work with other clients (non-competing)
- Moral rights to your contributions

---

### ⚠️ RESTRICTIONS (Limited & Defined)

**During & 12 Months After:**
- Don't solicit OMTA directly for competing services
- Don't use OMTA contact info obtained through this engagement for marketing
- Refer OMTA inquiries for Dale Carnegie services back to DC

**What You CAN Do:**
- General marketing (LinkedIn, newsletters, industry events)
- Work with other training firms and clients
- Accept non-competing consulting opportunities
- Continue existing business relationships

---

### 💰 COMPENSATION STRUCTURE

- **Your Rate**: $___ per hour / $___ per day (negotiated)
- **Payment**: Within 30 days of invoice
- **Expenses**: Pre-approved costs reimbursed
- **Tax Status**: 1099 (US) / T4A (Canada) - you're responsible for your own taxes

---

### 📝 INSURANCE REQUIREMENTS

You must maintain:
- Professional Liability (E&O): $2M minimum
- General Liability: $1M minimum
- DC named as additional insured

*This is standard for independent contractors in the training industry.*

---

### 🔒 DISPUTE RESOLUTION

If issues arise:
1. Good-faith negotiation (14 days)
2. Non-binding mediation (if needed)
3. Binding arbitration in Toronto (last resort)

*We want to resolve issues professionally, not litigiously.*

---

### ❓ FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

**Q: Can I work with other training companies while working with DC?**  
A: Yes. This is a non-exclusive partnership.

**Q: What if I already know someone at OMTA?**  
A: Disclose it upfront. Pre-existing relationships are typically fine; we just need transparency.

**Q: What happens if OMTA approaches me directly for more work?**  
A: Refer them to DC within 48 hours. DC manages the client relationship.

**Q: Who owns materials I create during the engagement?**  
A: DC owns work product created specifically for this engagement. You keep your pre-existing IP.

**Q: Can I use my own coaching exercises and assessments?**  
A: Yes, provided they don't compete with Dale Carnegie proprietary content.

**Q: What if I want to end the engagement early?**  
A: Either party can terminate with 14 days' written notice.

**Q: Is this an employment agreement?**  
A: No. You're an independent contractor responsible for your own taxes, insurance, and business expenses.
