## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY - THE BRUTAL TRUTH

**SUMMER IS NOT A GOOD TIME FOR EITHER BOOT CAMP CONCEPT.**

Both the General Boot Camp and Influence Boot Camp face significant headwinds during June-August:
- **Vacation season** dramatically reduces attendance probability
- **L&D budget cycles** mean most training decisions happen in Q1-Q2 for Q3-Q4 delivery
- **Venue costs** vary by location (can be higher OR lower)
- **Revenue per event** would need to be significantly discounted to fill seats

**RECOMMENDATION:** Skip summer boot camps entirely. Use summer for pilot testing, curriculum development, and building pipeline for a strong September launch.

However, if summer revenue is absolutely required, the **Influence Boot Camp has a slight edge** due to higher margins and corporate L&D's Q3 budget planning cycle.

## 2. CLIENT AVAILABILITY ANALYSIS

### Shared Services Professionals (Influence Boot Camp Target)

**Vacation Patterns:**
- **Professional services industry:** Most likely to vacation in June (Gusto data: >1-in-10 take June vacation)
- **HR, Finance, IT shared services:** Peak vacation July-August
- **50% coverage rule:** Most orgs ensure half their team is available - meaning 50% capacity max

**L&D Budget Cycles:**
| Quarter | L&D Activity | Implication |
|---------|--------------|-------------|
| Q1 (Jan-Mar) | Budget planning, vendor selection | High training bookings |
| Q2 (Apr-Jun) | Program delivery, budget execution | Peak training delivery |
| **Q3 (Jul-Sep)** | **Budget planning for NEXT year** | **Low immediate training spend** |
| Q4 (Oct-Dec) | Use-it-or-lose-it budget spend | High training bookings |

**Critical Finding:** Q3 (summer) is when L&D leaders plan for NEXT YEAR - not when they buy training for immediate delivery.

### General Boot Camp Target (Mass Market)

**Vacation Impact:**
- **July-August:** Families prioritize vacations over professional development
- **Competition:** Competes directly with summer activities, kids out of school
- **Registration patterns:** Typically 60-90 days out - summer events need spring signups (when focus is on finishing fiscal year)

## 3. INSTRUCTOR UTILIZATION

### Dale Carnegie Instructor Capacity
- **Current DC model:** Trainers receive 150+ hours certification
- **Delivery fee:** 10% of revenue ($4,500 per boot camp)
- **Capacity:** Instructors can typically handle 8-12 multi-day programs per year

### Summer Instructor Availability
**Key Question:** Can existing DC instructors deliver summer boot camps?

**Answer:** YES, with caveats:
- DC instructors are contractors/independent - they set their own schedules
- Many experienced trainers take summer vacation too
- **Premium required:** To secure instructors in summer, expect to pay 15-20% premium
- **Reality check:** DC instructors already command $3,000-5,000/day - summer premium pushes this to $3,500-6,000/day

### Alternative: Non-DC Instructors
- **Influence Boot Camp:** Requires subject matter expert in influence/persuasion
- **General Boot Camp:** Could use DC methodology instructors
- **Risk:** Non-DC instructors lack brand credibility

## 7. RISK ASSESSMENT: CANNIBALIZATION OF CORE BUSINESS

### Fall DCC Enrollment Risk

**General Boot Camp Risk: MODERATE to HIGH**
- Mass market positioning competes directly with Dale Carnegie Course (DCC)
- If priced at $250-350, creates price anchor that makes $2,900 DCC look expensive
- **Cannibalization probability:** 30-40% of General Boot Camp attendees might have enrolled in DCC
- **Revenue loss:** Each cannibalized DCC seat = $2,900 lost for $350 gained = **-$2,550 per occurrence**

**Influence Boot Camp Risk: LOW to MODERATE**
- Niche positioning (influence without authority) different from DCC's general leadership focus
- Target audience (shared services) may not be traditional DCC buyers
- **Cannibalization probability:** 10-15%
- **Mitigation:** Position as advanced/specialized complement to DCC, not replacement

### Brand Dilution Risk

**General Boot Camp:**
- Low-price positioning can dilute Dale Carnegie premium brand
- Risk of becoming known as "budget option"
- **Impact:** Long-term brand value erosion

**Influence Boot Camp:**
- Premium pricing ($500) maintains brand integrity
- Specialized positioning enhances brand (thought leadership)
- **Impact:** Positive brand association

**Influence Boot Camp:**
- Must generate **$10,000+ profit per month** to justify new program development

### Opportunity Cost Analysis

Every hour spent on summer boot camps is an hour NOT spent on:
- Fall DCC pipeline building (Sept-Dec is peak season)
- Corporate contract development (Q3 = Q4 planning)
- Curriculum development for premium programs

**Key Question:** Would you rather:
- A) Run summer boot camps that lose $6,000-14,000 over 3 months
- B) Use summer to build a $200,000+ fall pipeline

**Answer: B, every time.**

## 9. FINAL RECOMMENDATION

### PRIMARY RECOMMENDATION: NO SUMMER BOOT CAMPS

**Do not run either boot camp in summer.**

**Rationale:**
1. **Financial:** Both concepts project losses during summer months
2. **Strategic:** Diverts attention from core DCC business preparation for fall
3. **Brand risk:** General Boot Camp particularly risks brand dilution
4. **Market timing:** Fighting against vacation season is expensive and inefficient
5. **Opportunity cost:** Summer is better used for pipeline building

### ALTERNATIVE SUMMER STRATEGY

**Instead of boot camps, use summer for:**

1. **Pilot Testing** (July-August)
   - Run ONE subsidized Influence Boot Camp at $295
   - Invite 20 carefully selected prospects
   - Gather testimonials, refine content
   - Cost: ~$5,000 (marketing value: high)

2. **Corporate L&D Outreach** (June-August)
   - Target L&D leaders during their budget planning season
   - Position Influence Boot Camp for Q4 delivery
   - Goal: Secure 3-5 corporate contracts for fall

3. **Curriculum Development**
   - Finalize Influence Boot Camp content
   - Create marketing materials
   - Build landing pages and funnels

4. **Pipeline Building**
   - Email campaigns targeting September events
   - Early bird pricing for fall programs
   - Build waitlist for Q4

### IF FORCED TO CHOOSE ONE

**Choose Influence Boot Camp (barely):**
- Higher margins = lower volume requirement
- Corporate L&D budget cycles align with Q3
- Less cannibalization risk
- Better brand positioning

**BUT:** Only run it if:
- You have confirmed corporate sponsor/commitment
- You can secure 20+ pre-registrations by May 15
- You accept break-even as success (not profit)
