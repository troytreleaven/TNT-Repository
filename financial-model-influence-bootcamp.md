## 3. BREAK-EVEN ANALYSIS

### Break-Even Formula: Fixed Costs ÷ (Price - Variable Cost per Person)

### Option A: $250/person
| Attendees | Revenue | Variable Costs | Fixed Costs (Mid) | Profit/Loss | Margin |
|-----------|---------|----------------|-------------------|-------------|--------|
| 15 | $3,750 | $1,800 | $8,800 | **-$6,850** | -183% |
| 20 | $5,000 | $2,400 | $8,800 | **-$6,200** | -124% |
| 25 | $6,250 | $3,000 | $8,800 | **-$5,550** | -89% |
| 30 | $7,500 | $3,600 | $8,800 | **-$4,900** | -65% |
| 35 | $8,750 | $4,200 | $8,800 | **-$4,250** | -49% |
| 40 | $10,000 | $4,800 | $8,800 | **-$3,600** | -36% |
| **BREAK-EVEN** | 64 | $16,000 | $7,680 | $8,800 | **$0** | 0% |

**⚠️ VERDICT: $250 PRICE IS NOT VIABLE**
- Break-even requires 64 attendees (unrealistic for 1-day boot camp)
- Even at 40 attendees, you lose $3,600

### Option B: $400/person
| Attendees | Revenue | Variable Costs | Fixed Costs (Mid) | Profit/Loss | Margin |
|-----------|---------|----------------|-------------------|-------------|--------|

| 35 | $14,000 | $4,200 | $8,800 | **$1,000** | 7% |
| 40 | $16,000 | $4,800 | $8,800 | **$2,400** | 15% |

**VERDICT: $400 PRICE IS MARGINAL**
- Break-even at 30 attendees (achievable but tight)
- 40 attendees = $2,400 profit (15% margin)
- Risky if attendance drops below 30

### B. Virtual Delivery Economics

| Cost Component | In-Person | Virtual |
|----------------|-----------|---------|
| Venue | $1,500 | $0 |
| Instructor travel | $1,000 | $0 |

**Virtual Pricing Reality Check:**
- Market pays 30-50% LESS for virtual ($250-$350)

- Profit: $10,500 - $7,000 - $2,100 = **$1,400**
- Margin: 13% (vs 17% in-person at $500)

**VERDICT: Virtual is viable but significantly less profitable**

## 6. RISK FACTORS & MITIGATION

### Risk Matrix
| Risk | Probability | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|-------------|--------|------------|
| **Low attendance (15 people)** | MEDIUM | SEVERE | Early bird pricing, cancellation thresholds |
| **No-shows (20% typical)** | HIGH | MODERATE | Overbook by 10%, non-refundable policy |
| **Instructor cancellation** | LOW | SEVERE | Backup trainer, cancellation insurance |
| **Venue double-booking** | LOW | SEVERE | Signed contract, backup venues identified |
| **Negative reviews** | MEDIUM | HIGH | Beta test, gather testimonials first |
| **Competitive response** | MEDIUM | MODERATE | Differentiate on outcomes, not just content |

### Minimum Viable Numbers (GO/NO-GO Thresholds)

| Price Point | Minimum Viable Attendees | Action if Below |
|-------------|-------------------------|-----------------|
| $400 | 30 attendees | Cancel/postpone with full refunds |
| $500 | 24 attendees | Run at breakeven for testimonials |
| $500 | 20 attendees | Cancel - lose $1,200 vs lose more |

### Cancellation Insurance Options
| Policy Type | Cost | Coverage | Recommendation |
|-------------|------|----------|----------------|
| Event cancellation | $300-500 | Weather, venue issues | YES - Worth it |
| Instructor disability | $200-400 | Key person unable to perform | YES if sole instructor |
| Attendance shortfall | N/A (rarely available) | Revenue loss | Build into pricing instead |

### Worst Case Scenario: 15 Attendees @ $500
- Revenue: $7,500
- Costs: $1,800 (variable) + $8,800 (fixed) = $10,600
- **Loss: $3,100**
- Plus launch costs amortization: ~$800
- **Total loss per failed event: $3,900**

**How many failures can you absorb?**
- With 2 successful events (30 @ $500 = $5,200 profit each)
- You can absorb **2-3 failed events** before launch costs unrecovered
