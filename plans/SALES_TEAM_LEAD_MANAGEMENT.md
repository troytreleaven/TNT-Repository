### 1.2 Lead Routing Rules

**Tier 1 - Hot Leads (Immediate Assignment):**
- Referral from existing client
- Workshop attendee who indicated interest
- Company that has sent people to our programs before
- Inbound from website with budget mentioned
- **Assignment:** Round-robin to available reps

**Tier 2 - Warm Leads (24-48 hour follow-up):**
- Downloaded lead magnet
- Attended webinar
- LinkedIn engagement
- **Assignment:** By territory

**Tier 3 - Cold Leads (Batch Processing):**
- PhantomBuster extracted leads
- Trade show scans
- Research-based prospects
- **Assignment:** Weekly batch distribution

### 2.2 Follow-Up Cadence

**Hot Leads (Tier 1):**
- Day 0: Initial call + email same day
- Day 1: Follow-up call
- Day 3: Third attempt
- Day 7: Fourth attempt + voicemail
- Day 14: Final attempt + email
- Day 30: Re-engagement email (if still interested)

**Warm Leads (Tier 2):**
- Day 0: Initial email
- Day 3: First call attempt
- Day 7: Second call attempt
- Day 14: Third attempt
- Day 30: Final attempt

**Cold Leads (Tier 3):**
- Day 0: Email sequence start
- Day 7: First call
- Day 21: Second call
- Day 45: Final attempt

### 2.3 Response Time Standards

| Lead Type | First Response | Follow-up Response |
|-----------|----------------|-------------------|
| Hot | < 2 hours | < 4 hours |
| Warm | < 24 hours | < 24 hours |
| Cold | < 48 hours | < 48 hours |

## Part 8: Lead Quality Scoring

### Scoring Model (1-10)

**Company Factors:**
- Company size (5-500+ employees): +1 to +3
- Industry (our sweet spot): +1 to +2
- Budget approved: +2
- Timeline (< 3 months): +2

**Contact Factors:**
- Decision maker: +2
- Has bought before: +2
- Receptive on first contact: +1 to +2
- Multiple contacts in company: +1

**Score Interpretation:**
- 8-10: Hot - Immediate priority
- 5-7: Warm - Standard follow-up
- 1-4: Cold - Nurture sequence only

## Appendix B: Phone Scripts

### Opening Script

```
"Hi [Name], this is [Name] from Dale Carnegie Training. Do you have 2 minutes?

I'm reaching out because we've been helping companies in [Industry] develop their leaders, and I wanted to see if that's something [Company Name] might be looking at this year.

What's your current approach to leadership development?"
```

### Handling Objections

**"We're not interested"**
"I understand. What would need to change for that to be a priority?"

**"We already have a provider"**
"That's great - who are you working with? What are you most happy with? What would you improve?"

**"Send me information"**
"Absolutely. What specific areas are you most interested in so I can tailor what I send?"
