# 1. CORPORATE MARKET ANALYSIS

## Market Size & Dynamics

| Segment | 2026 Market Size | Growth Rate |
|---------|-----------------|-------------|
| Leadership Training | $40.8B | 8.4% CAGR |
| Executive Coaching | $113B | 9.1% CAGR |
| L&D Total Spend | $101.8B (2024-25) | Steady growth |
| Per-Employee Spend | $1,071 average | Up from $874 in 2024 |

**Key trend:** L&D leaders must now "act as business partners who prove their financial and strategic impact" (Training Industry 2025). They're under pressure to demonstrate ROI, not just run programs.

## Who Are the Buyers?

**Primary Decision Makers:**
1. **L&D Directors/VPs** - Own the training budget, evaluate vendors, measure outcomes
2. **HR VPs** - Broader workforce development, approve large contracts
3. **Department Heads** - Have their own budgets for team development (easier entry point)
4. **COO/Operations** - Want productivity gains, often control pilot budgets

**The Buying Committee:**
- 67% of enterprise training purchases involve 2-5 stakeholders
- Procurement often gets involved for contracts >$10K
- Finance reviews ROI claims

## What Do They Want?

**Top L&D Priorities for 2026 (TalentLMS/Gartner):**
1. Skills gaps with business impact (#1)
2. Talent retention
3. Training effectiveness measurement
4. ROI demonstration to C-suite
5. Leadership development (64% of orgs prioritizing)

**What they DON'T want:**
- Generic "communication skills" training
- One-size-fits-all programs
- Vendors who add complexity to their job
- Anything they can't measure

## Decision Process

**Typical corporate training sales cycle:**
- **Pilot/Initial deal:** 3-6 months
- **Enterprise contract:** 6-12 months
- **Budget cycle:** FY-aligned (often Q4 for next year)
- **Proof required:** Case studies, ROI calculators, references

**Key insight:** They buy based on **specific pain**, not general development. "Influence without authority" is a recognized category. "Business acumen" is not.

# 3. CORPORATE SALES STRATEGY

## How to Sell to L&D

**The L&D Persona:**
- Overwhelmed with vendors
- Must justify spend to CFO/CEO
- Want "trusted advisor," not "vendor"
- Measure: completion rates, behavior change, business outcomes

**Messaging Framework:**

**Primary message:** "Transform Order-Takers into Strategic Partners"

**Avoid:** Generic "communication skills" or "leadership development"
**Use:** Specific pain points—"influence without authority," "matrix organization challenges," "getting buy-in without authority"

**Key tactics:**
1. Lead with their pain, not your solution
2. Offer measurement from day 1
3. Build relationship before pitch
4. Provide case studies, not just testimonials

## Decision Makers

| Role | What They Care About | How to Reach |
|------|---------------------|--------------|
| L&D Director | Program effectiveness, ROI, ease of administration | LinkedIn, industry events, content marketing |
| HR VP | Talent retention, culture, workforce development | Executive networking, referrals |
| Department Head | Team performance, specific skills | Direct outreach, pilot offers |
| CFO/Finance | ROI, cost justification | Case studies with numbers |

## Sales Cycle Management

**Month 1-2:** Research + relationship building
- Understand their pain
- Share relevant case studies
- No pitch yet

**Month 2-3:** Solution exploration
- Propose pilot or assessment
- Align on success metrics
- Involve procurement if needed

**Month 3-6:** Pilot execution
- Deliver measurable results
- Gather testimonials
- Plan expansion

**Month 6+:** Expansion & renewal
- Formal contract
- Annual retainer discussions
- Referrals to other departments

**Recommended corporate proposal outline:**

6. Next steps & timeline

**Key differentiator:** Include success metrics upfront. "This program will result in X% improvement in stakeholder alignment" etc.

# 5. PILOT PROGRAMS

## How to Get in the Door

**Pilot program structure:**
- **Duration:** 1 day (6-8 hours) or 2 half-days
- **Size:** 10-15 participants
- **Price:** $5,000-8,000 (or free for strategic accounts)
- **Outcome:** Pre/post assessment, immediate action items

**Pilot objectives:**
1. Prove the methodology works
2. Create internal advocates
3. Generate case study material
4. Open expansion conversations

## Pilot Value

| Pilot Investment | Typical Expansion | ROI |
|-----------------|-------------------|-----|
| $5K pilot | $15-25K expansion | 3-5x |
| $8K pilot | $25-50K annual | 3-6x |
| Free strategic pilot | $50K+ enterprise | N/A |

**Key to expansion:** Measure and report. "Participants improved influence score by 40% in 30 days."

## Pilot-to-Expansion Playbook

**Week 1-2:** Deliver pilot, gather feedback
**Week 3:** Present results to stakeholders
**Month 2:** Propose team license (5-10 people)
**Month 3-4:** Expand to department
**Month 6:** Enterprise-wide conversation

# 8. WINNING FORMULA

## What Makes Us Win Corporate Deals

**1. Specificity over generality**
- "Influence without authority" wins over "leadership development"
- Target specific functions: IT, HR Shared Services, PMO

**2. Measurement from day one**
- Pre/post assessments
- 30/60/90 day follow-up
- ROI calculators

**3. Trusted advisor positioning**
- Don't sell programs; solve problems
- Ask questions, listen 70%
- Share relevant insights, not just pitches

**4. Pilot strategy**
- Lower risk entry
- Prove before scaling
- Create internal advocates

**5. Modern alternative positioning**
- Dale Carnegie = dated but trusted
- Position as "modern version" for evolving workplaces
- Hybrid/digital delivery they lack
