## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

### VERDICT: **GO - With Strategic Positioning Adjustments**

The influence/persuasion training market is a **validated, proven category** with strong demand indicators. However, this is **NOT a blue ocean**—significant competition exists from established players. The opportunity lies in a **specific underserved niche**: mid-level shared services professionals who need influence skills but are priced out of premium executive programs and underwhelmed by generic online courses.

### Key Findings at a Glance:
- Market Size: $22.8B (US soft skills training, 2024) with 5.87% CAGR
- Target Segment: 37-42% of global market is North America
- Competition: Established but gaps exist in one-day intensive format for mid-tier professionals
- Price Point Opportunity: Sweet spot between $500-$1,500 for one-day intensive
- Search Demand: "Influence without authority" is a high-intent, established training category

## 2. KEY COMPETITORS ANALYSIS

### Tier 1: Premium Executive Education

#### Columbia Business School - "Persuasion: Influencing Without Authority"
- **Format:** 3-day in-person program
- **Price:** ~$6,500-$8,500 (estimated based on comparable programs)
- **Audience:** Senior executives, C-suite
- **Positioning:** Academic rigor, peer networking, Ivy League credential
- **Strengths:** Brand prestige, research-based, comprehensive curriculum
- **Weaknesses:** High cost, time-intensive, elite-focused

#### Wharton Executive Education - "Executive Influence"
- **Format:** Multi-day on-campus
- **Price:** $8,000-$12,000+
- **Audience:** Senior executives
- **Positioning:** Elite business school credential

#### Harvard DCE - "Influence and Persuasion in Leadership"
- **Format:** Multi-week program
- **Price:** Premium tier
- **Audience:** Managers with 2+ years experience

## 4. MARKET GAPS & OPPORTUNITIES

### Identified Gaps

#### 1. **The "Missing Middle"**
- **Gap:** Mid-level professionals (manager to director level) in shared services need influence skills but:
  - Can't afford $6K+ executive programs
  - Don't have time for 8-week Dale Carnegie courses
  - Find online courses too generic/unaccountable
- **Opportunity:** One-day intensive for this specific segment

#### 2. **Shared Services Specificity**
- **Gap:** Most influence training is generic or sales-focused
- **Opportunity:** Tailored for HR, IT, Finance, Marketing shared services
  - Cross-functional collaboration challenges
  - Getting buy-in from business partners
  - Influencing without direct authority
  - "Managing up" in matrix organizations

#### 3. **Immediate Application Format**
- **Gap:** Multi-week programs lose momentum; online lacks urgency
- **Opportunity:** One-day immersive "boot camp" format
  - Intensive skill-building
  - Real scenarios from participants
  - Actionable toolkit to use Monday morning

#### 4. **Live Interaction at Accessible Price**
- **Gap:** $29/month online courses lack engagement; $6K programs are exclusive
- **Opportunity:** Live, instructor-led at $1,000-$1,500 price point

### What's NOT Being Served Well

| Need | Current Market Status |
|------|----------------------|
| One-day influence intensive for mid-level pros | Limited options (TrainSMART mentioned but not prominent) |
| Shared services-specific scenarios | No dedicated programs found |
| Live interaction at sub-$2,000 price | Gap between online ($30/month) and premium ($6K+) |
| Influence WITHOUT sales focus | Most courses are sales-persuasion oriented |
