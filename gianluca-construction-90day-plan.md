# GIANLUCA CONSTRUCTION DIVISION
## 90-Day "PROVE IT" Battle Plan
### Validation-First Approach | Revised February 2026

---

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

**The Hard Truth:** The original plan assumed a $10K/participant COJG grant that no longer exists. Without "FREE" as the value prop, we're selling on merit alone—and we haven't proven we can do that yet.

**The Pivot:** This plan replaces the "build it and they will come" approach with a **$15,000 validation test** that either proves market demand or kills the initiative before major capital is committed.

**The Goal:** In 90 days, determine with 90% confidence whether this opportunity is real or imaginary.

---

## ADDRESSING THE SCEPTIC'S OBJECTIONS

| Objection | Our Response |
|-----------|--------------|
| **COJG pause removes "FREE"** | Acknowledged. We price for market reality, not grant dependency. |
| **$3K pricing suicide** | New pricing: $995-$1,495 (self-pay viable range) |
| **Gianluca unproven** | 30-day "can he sell?" test before any major commitment |
| **One-person risk** | Revenue share model, not salary. He only earns if it works. |
| **$250 CAC fantasy** | Revised to $800-1,200 with validation tracking |
| **8-week commitment unrealistic** | New format: 2-day intensive + 4-week reinforcement |
| **175 participants delusional** | Target: 15-25 pilots in 90 days, 40-60 Year 1 if validated |
| **No validation** | This entire plan IS the validation |

## REVISED OFFER: "CONSTRUCTION LEADERSHIP ACCELERATOR"

| Original | Revised | Rationale |
|----------|---------|-----------|
| 8 weekly sessions | 2-day intensive + 4 weekly 90-min reinforcement calls | Construction schedules are chaotic; intensive format respects their reality |
| 2.5 hours/session | Day 1: 8am-4pm, Day 2: 8am-12pm | Immersive experience, less calendar fragmentation |
| 20 participants/cohort | 8-12 participants/cohort | Smaller = more intimate, easier to fill, higher perceived value |
| $3,000/participant | $1,295/participant | Self-pay viable; 60% gross margin achievable |
| In-person only | Hybrid option (in-person preferred, virtual available) | Expands geographic reach, reduces venue risk |

- Human Relations Principles for construction sites

**4-Week Reinforcement:**
- Weekly 90-min video calls (recorded for those who miss)
- Peer accountability groups
- Job-specific application assignments
- Gianluca office hours (optional)

**Ongoing Support:**
- Private LinkedIn/Slack group
- Monthly "Construction Leadership Roundtable" (free, lead gen)
- Resource library (templates, checklists, scripts)

## GIANLUCA READINESS TEST

Before investing a single dollar in marketing, Gianluca must pass the **"Can He Sell?"** test.

### Test 1: The Warm-Up Round (Days 1-10)

**Objective:** Can Gianluca generate 5 qualified conversations from his existing network?

**Activities:**
- Identify 30 contacts in construction (LinkedIn, phone, past colleagues)
- Schedule 15-minute "validation conversations" (not sales calls)
- Use script: *"I'm exploring launching a leadership program specifically for construction. Would you be open to a brief conversation about the challenges you see?"*

**Pass Criteria:**
- [ ] 5+ conversations completed
- [ ] 3+ expressed genuine interest in the problem
- [ ] 1+ said "I'd pay for that" unprompted
- [ ] Gianluca demonstrates he can schedule and conduct business conversations

**Investment:** $0 (time only)

**Fail Scenario:** If Gianluca can't get 5 conversations in 10 days, he's not ready. Pause and reassess.

---

### Test 2: The Pitch Test (Days 11-20)

**Objective:** Can Gianluca credibly present the offer and handle objections?

**Activities:**
- Gianluca practices pitch with 3 Dale Carnegie team members
- Conducts 3 "beta sales calls" with warm construction contacts
- Records at least 1 call for review (with permission)

**Pass Criteria:**
- [ ] Pitch is clear, confident, and benefits-focused
- [ ] Handles "how much?" question without flinching
- [ ] Gets to next step ("send me details" or "let me think about it") consistently
- [ ] Dale Carnegie leadership approves pitch quality

**Investment:** $500 (Dale Carnegie staff time for practice)

**Fail Scenario:** If Gianluca can't credibly pitch after 10 days of coaching, he's not the right person for this role.

---

### Test 3: The Commitment Test (Days 21-30)

**Objective:** Can Gianluca close paying customers?

**Activities:**
- Offer "founding cohort" discount ($995 vs. $1,295)
- Target: 3 paid deposits to run first pilot
- Full sales cycle execution (prospect → conversation → proposal → close)

**Pass Criteria:**
- [ ] 3+ paid deposits secured (total: $2,985-$4,485)
- [ ] Minimum 8 total commits (including "soft commits")
- [ ] Gianluca demonstrates follow-through and closing ability

**Investment:** $1,000 (marketing materials, landing page, Gianluca time)

**Fail Scenario:** If fewer than 3 paid deposits after 30 days, kill or pivot the program.

## KILL CRITERIA: WHEN TO WALK AWAY

### Hard Kills (Immediate Termination)

| Trigger | Rationale |
|---------|-----------|
| **Gianluca fails Readiness Test 1** (can't get 5 conversations) | Fundamental network/sales capability issue |
| **Fewer than 3 paid deposits by Day 30** | Market doesn't want this at this price |
| **CAC tracking >$1,500** | Unit economics don't work |
| **Pilot completion rate <50%** | Product-market fit failure |
| **Pilot NPS <20** | Customers don't see value |
| **Multiple refund requests** | Delivery or expectation problem |
| **Gianluca quits or unavailable** | One-person dependency realized |

### Soft Kills (Consider Pivot First)

| Trigger | Pivot Options |
|---------|---------------|
| Price resistance at $1,295 | Test $995 or $795 price points |
| 2-day format doesn't work | Try 1-day intensive or virtual series |
| Individuals buying, not companies | B2C model with different pricing |
| Low volume but high satisfaction | Keep as boutique offering, don't scale |
| Gianluca strong at delivery, weak at sales | Hire/partner for sales |
| Demand in different region | Virtual delivery or different territory |

### Decision Framework

**Day 30 Check:** If fewer than 3 paid deposits, pause. Options:
1. Lower price and try again (another 2 weeks)
2. Change format and try again
3. Kill the program

**Day 60 Check:** If pilot NPS <30, don't scale. Options:
1. Fix delivery issues and run another pilot
2. Pivot format/content
3. Kill the program

**Day 90 Check:** If no path to 40+ participants in Year 1, don't commit resources. Options:
1. Keep as small "side hustle" for Gianluca
2. Merge with existing Dale Carnegie programs
3. Kill the program

## ALTERNATIVE OUTCOMES (IF FULL PROGRAM FAILS)

If the full program doesn't validate, consider these pivots before killing entirely:

### Option A: The "Lunch & Learn" Model
- Free 90-minute session at construction associations
- Lead generation for 1:1 coaching or consulting
- Lower revenue but lower risk

### Option B: Corporate Consulting
- Skip public programs
- Direct sales to construction companies
- Custom training for their specific needs
- Higher ticket ($5K-15K), fewer sales needed

### Option C: Content & Community
- LinkedIn newsletter + podcast for construction leaders
- Monetize through sponsorships, not tuition

- License content to construction associations

## CONCLUSION: THE BET WE'RE MAKING

**Original Plan:** Bet $100K+ on unproven assumptions

**This Plan:** Bet $12,500 to prove or disprove those assumptions in 90 days

**The Worst Case:** We spend $12,500 and 90 days to learn that this market doesn't exist at this price point. We kill the program and avoid a $100K mistake.

**The Most Likely Case:** We learn a lot, adjust the model, and either have a smaller but viable opportunity OR a clear reason to pivot/kill.
