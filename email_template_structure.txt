## 2. OPENING HOOK OPTIONS

**Option A — The Reality Check:**
"You've tried the workshops. Sent the team to the seminars. Yet somehow, Monday morning looks the same as it did last year. What if the problem isn't your people — it's the approach?"

**Option B — The Contrarian:**
"Most leadership training is forgotten by Friday. Not because your managers don't care — because it wasn't built for real-world pressure. There's a different way, and it starts with one decision."

**Option C — The Scenario:**
"Picture this: Your team tackles problems without you. Conflicts resolve before they reach your desk. Your best people stay — and bring their friends. That's not luck. That's a system."

## 3. BODY STRUCTURE TEMPLATE

### [PAIN_POINT_SECTION]
(PARAGRAPH 1 — 2-3 sentences max)
Acknowledge a specific, relatable pain point the business owner faces:
- High turnover despite competitive pay
- Managers who avoid difficult conversations
- Training that doesn't stick
- Teams that wait to be told what to do

**Example:**
"You've invested in training before. Your managers sat through workshops, took notes, even seemed motivated. But back at the office? Old habits returned within weeks."

### [SOLUTION_SECTION]
(PARAGRAPH 2 — 2-3 sentences max)
Introduce the solution without giving the full pitch. Focus on the *outcome*, not the process:

**Example:**
"Dale Carnegie training works because it rewires behavior, not just knowledge. Our graduates don't just understand leadership — they practice it until it becomes automatic."

### [SOCIAL_PROOF]
(PARAGRAPH 3 — 1-2 sentences OR bullet points)
Use one of these formats:

**Format A — Single Stat:**
"One manufacturing client cut supervisor turnover by 47% within 18 months."

**Format B — Testimonial Snippet:**
"'For the first time, my team is solving problems instead of bringing them to me.' — Operations Director, [Industry]"

**Format C — Bullet Proof:**
• 4,000+ companies trained annually  
• 89% of graduates report improved team performance  
• Used by 400+ of the Fortune 500

### [EVENT_DETAILS]

**See it in action:**

**Date:** [Day, Month Date, Year]  
**Time:** [Start Time] — [End Time]  
**Format:** [In-person/Virtual]  
**Investment:** [Free/Complimentary]

### [CTA]
(Final paragraph — ONE clear action)

**Strong:**

**Alternative:**
**[Claim your spot (space is limited) →]**

## 4. FORMATTING GUIDELINES

### Paragraph Length
- Maximum 2-3 sentences per paragraph
- Single idea per paragraph
- No walls of text

### White Space Rules
- Blank line between every paragraph
- Blank line before event details
- Extra spacing after CTA before signature

### Text Emphasis
- **Bold** for key outcomes and CTAs
- Use sparingly — 2-3 bold elements maximum
- Never bold entire paragraphs

### Bullet Points
- Use only for social proof section
- Maximum 3 bullets
- Lead with the result/number

### Mobile Optimization
- Subject line: 40 characters or less (test on mobile preview)
- Sender name should be a person, not "noreply"
- Preheader text: 80-100 characters
- Links: large tap targets, spaced apart
- Preview on iPhone and Android before sending

### Signature Block
```
—

[First Name]
[Title], Dale Carnegie [Location]
[Email] | [Phone]
[LinkedIn URL — optional]

P.S. [Optional: Add urgency or secondary benefit]
```

## 5. TONE CHECKLIST

Before sending, verify:

**Conversational, not corporate:**
- ❌ "We would like to extend an invitation..."
- ✅ "I'd like to invite you to..."
- ❌ "Leverage our proven methodology..."
- ✅ "Here's what actually works..."

**Confident, not arrogant:**
- ❌ "We're the best in the business."
- ✅ "We train 4,000+ companies a year. Here's why they keep coming back."
- ❌ "You need this training."
- ✅ "If you're tired of training that doesn't stick, this is worth 90 minutes."

**Urgent, not desperate:**
- ❌ "Please respond ASAP!"
- ✅ "6 seats available. First come, first served."
- ❌ "Don't miss this opportunity!"
- ✅ "If this resonates, grab a spot."

**Professional, not stiff:**
- ❌ "Dear Sir/Madam,"
- ✅ "Hi [First Name],"
- ❌ "We remain at your disposal..."
- ✅ "Questions? Just reply to this email."

## 7. PLACEHOLDER CHEAT SHEET

**[PAIN_POINT_SECTION]:**  
Describe 1-2 specific frustrations the recipient likely experiences. Be specific enough to feel personal, broad enough to apply to most business owners.

**[SOLUTION_SECTION]:**  
The "what" without the "how." Promise an outcome, not a curriculum. Keep it under 40 words.

**[SOCIAL_PROOF]:**  
One compelling stat, quote, or credential. Specificity beats generic praise.

**[EVENT_DETAILS]:**  
Date, time, format, cost. Use bold headers. Separate with white space.

**[CTA]:**  
Single action. Button or bold link. No secondary CTAs.
