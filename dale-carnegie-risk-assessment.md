# RISK MITIGATION STRATEGIES

## FOR GENERAL BOOT CAMP (If Proceeding)

### Pre-Launch Requirements
1. **Minimum Viable Test:** Pre-sell 15 seats before building curriculum. If can't sell 15, kill it immediately.
2. **Partner or Perish:** Secure venue partnership (hotel, university, WeWork) to reduce fixed costs. Don't sign venue contracts until 20 seats pre-sold.
3. **Facilitator Lock:** Confirm 2 trained facilitators can commit to monthly schedule. Single point of failure otherwise.

### Launch Phase
4. **Launch with Loss Leader:** First event priced at $299 (or free for strategic accounts) to prove concept and generate testimonials.
5. **Guarantee or GTFO:** Offer money-back guarantee to reduce buyer friction. Track refund rate religiously.
6. **Corporate Pre-Sales:** Secure 1-2 corporate sponsors who buy blocks of seats before marketing to individuals.

### Ongoing
7. **Kill Criteria:** Define clear metrics to kill the program (see Decision Framework below).
8. **Marketing Budget Cap:** Limit marketing spend to $5K for first event. If CAC exceeds $100/seat, pivot or kill.

## FOR INFLUENCE BOOT CAMP (If Ignoring This Advice)

*Note: These mitigations address symptoms, not the fatal market mismatch. Recommend abandoning this concept.*

1. **Rename Immediately:** "Boot camp" kills credibility at $1,500+. Call it "Influence Intensive" or "Executive Sprint."
2. **Pilot with Existing Client:** Test concept with current Dale Carnegie corporate client before public launch.
3. **Consulting Wrapper:** Position as discovery workshop leading to larger consulting engagement, not standalone product.
4. **RFP Response:** Build as response to specific client RFP, not speculative product development.

# DECISION FRAMEWORK: WHEN TO KILL

## GENERAL BOOT CAMP KILL CRITERIA

**Kill Immediately If:**
- [ ] Cannot pre-sell 15 seats for first event
- [ ] No qualified facilitator committed within 30 days
- [ ] Marketing CAC exceeds $150/seat

**Kill After First Event If:**
- [ ] Net Promoter Score < 40
- [ ] Refund requests > 10%
- [ ] < 20% of attendees express interest in high-ticket programs
- [ ] Cannot secure 2nd event venue partnership

**Kill After 90 Days If:**
- [ ] Cumulative revenue < $30K
- [ ] < 3 high-ticket sales generated from attendees
- [ ] Burn rate exceeds $15K/month with no path to profitability

**Pivot Trigger:** If individual sales fail but corporate block sales emerge, pivot entirely to B2B model.

## INFLUENCE BOOT CAMP KILL CRITERIA

**Don't Start If:**
- [ ] No existing shared services client willing to pilot
- [ ] Cannot secure facilitator with 5+ years shared services experience
- [ ] Sales cycle research shows > 6 months average close time

**Kill Immediately If Started:**
- [ ] First prospecting cycle yields < 3 qualified leads
- [ ] Pilot client expresses format concerns
- [ ] Any competitor launches similar offering at lower price

# THE BRUTAL TRUTH

Neither boot camp concept is a slam dunk. Both carry significant execution and market risks. But Troy's $300K/month goal demands aggressive action with acceptable risk profiles.

**The General Boot Camp is the only viable option.** It's a volume play with a proven demand format (intensive workshops) and a clear path to $30-100K/month if executed well.

**The Influence Boot Camp is a distraction.** It solves a problem Troy doesn't have (access to shared services market) with a solution the market doesn't want (expensive boot camps). Kill it.

**My honest assessment:** Even the General Boot Camp is a 50/50 proposition. Troy should launch it ONLY with strict pre-launch validation (15 pre-sales minimum) and ruthless kill criteria. If he can't pre-sell 15 seats, he should abandon boot camps entirely and invest in proven revenue drivers (high-ticket sales, digital courses, partner channels).

The $300K goal is achievable, but not by chasing every idea. Disciplined execution of proven concepts beats speculative innovation every time.
