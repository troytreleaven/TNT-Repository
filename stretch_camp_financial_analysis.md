- Expected attendance: -30-35% vs $250

- Only viable in Downtown Toronto/Mississauga

## 6. RISK ASSESSMENT

### 6.1 Risk Matrix

| Risk Factor | Probability | Impact | Mitigation Strategy |
|-------------|------------|--------|---------------------|
| Low Enrollment (<30) | Medium | High | Early marketing, group discounts, cancellation clause |
| Venue Cancellation | Low | High | Backup venues, contract terms |
| Instructor Unavailable | Low | Medium | 2-3 qualified backups on retainer |
| Weather (outdoor marketing) | Medium | Low | Indoor backup plans, digital focus |
| Economic Downturn | Medium | High | Lower price tier options, corporate packages |
| Competition Event | Medium | Medium | Date flexibility, unique positioning |
| Technical Issues | Low | Medium | AV checks, backup equipment |
| Food Service Issues | Low | Medium | Pre-approved caterers, dietary surveys |

### 6.2 Minimum Enrollment Requirements

**CRITICAL THRESHOLDS:**

| Scenario | Minimum Attendees | Revenue | Net Position (Medium Costs) | Action |
|----------|------------------|---------|---------------------------|--------|
| **GO/PROCEED** | 35+ | $8,750+ | Near break-even or profit | Run event |
| **CAUTION** | 28-34 | $7,000-$8,500 | ($1,000)-($2,000) loss | Consider options* |
| **CANCEL** | <28 | <$7,000 | >$2,000 loss | Cancel/reschedule |

**Options when in CAUTION zone:**
1. Reduce food costs (simpler lunch)
2. Move to lower-cost venue
3. Negotiate lower instructor fee
4. Offer last-minute deep discounts
5. Reschedule to better date

### 6.3 Cancellation Policies

**Recommended Structure:**

**Event Cancellation by Organizer:**
```
14+ days before: Full refund to all registrants
7-13 days before: 50% refund OR full credit for future event
0-6 days before: Full credit for future event only
```

**Attendee Cancellation:**
```
14+ days before: Full refund minus $25 admin fee
7-13 days before: 50% refund OR full credit
0-6 days before: No refund, credit for future event only
No-show: No refund, no credit
```

**Force Majeure:**
- Full refund or credit option
- Rescheduling priority

### 6.4 Risk Mitigation Strategies

**Enrollment Risk:**
- Launch marketing 8 weeks before event
- Set "go/no-go" decision date at 3 weeks out (need 25 confirmed)
- Offer "early bird" pricing to drive early commitment
- Partner with 3-5 corporate sponsors for guaranteed minimums

**Financial Risk:**
- Never book high-cost venues without 30+ confirmed registrations
- Negotiate refundable deposits where possible
- Build $2,000 contingency fund per event
- Consider event insurance for large-scale events

**Operational Risk:**
- Maintain database of 3 qualified instructors per region
- Have backup venue options researched
- Require signed contracts with caterers 2 weeks out
- Conduct tech checks day before event

### 6.5 What Could Go Wrong - Detailed Analysis

**1. Enrollment Shortfall (Probability: 30%)**
- Impact: Loss of $2,000-$5,000 per event
- Causes: Poor timing, competing events, weak marketing
- Prevention: Strong early campaign, flexible dates

**2. Cost Overruns (Probability: 25%)**
- Impact: 15-25% budget overrun
- Causes: Last-minute venue changes, higher F&B costs
- Prevention: Fixed-price contracts, 10% contingency buffer

**3. Instructor Issues (Probability: 10%)**
- Impact: Event cancellation or quality issues
- Causes: Illness, scheduling conflicts
- Prevention: Backup instructor network

**4. Low Quality Experience (Probability: 15%)**
- Impact: Poor reviews, damage to Dale Carnegie brand
- Causes: Inadequate prep, venue problems
- Prevention: Thorough rehearsals, quality checks

**5. Weather/Seasonal Issues (Probability: 20% for summer)**
- Impact: Reduced attendance
- Causes: Heat waves, competing summer activities
- Prevention: Air-conditioned venues, attractive indoor value proposition
