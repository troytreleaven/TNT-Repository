# THE INFLUENCE BOOT CAMP: CURRICULUM FEASIBILITY ASSESSMENT

## Executive Summary: VERDICT = CONDITIONAL GO ⚠️

**One day CAN work** for creating foundational influence skills with shared services professionals, BUT with critical caveats:
- Must be highly focused on APPLICATION over theory
- Pre-work is non-negotiable
- Post-work reinforcement is essential
- Class size must be limited (max 20 participants)
- Participants will leave with "conscious competence" (awareness + initial tools), not mastery

**Bottom line:** It's a strong START, not a complete solution. Think "intensive immersion + ongoing practice" not "one-and-done."

---

## 1. CURRICULUM OUTLINE: Hour-by-Hour Breakdown

### PRE-WORK (Required Before Attending)
**Time: 60-90 minutes** | **Format: Self-paced online modules**

1. **Stakeholder Mapping Exercise** - Participants identify 3 real influence challenges they currently face
2. **Influence Self-Assessment** - 20-question diagnostic on current influence style and gaps
3. **Case Study Pre-Read** - One brief case study of influence success/failure in shared services context

*Why this matters:* Every minute of pre-work saves 3 minutes of class time. Non-negotiable for one-day format.

## 3. DELIVERY METHOD ASSESSMENT

### Can This Be Taught Effectively in One Day?

**Answer: YES, but with important qualifiers:**

| Aspect | One-Day Feasibility | Notes |
|--------|---------------------|-------|
| **Awareness Building** | ✅ Excellent | Participants will understand WHAT to do |
| **Initial Skill Building** | ✅ Good | Participants will practice each core skill once |
| **Behavioral Change** | ⚠️ Moderate | Requires follow-through; one day starts the habit |
| **Mastery** | ❌ Not Possible | Mastery requires 6-12 months of practice |
| **Confidence** | ✅ Good | Participants leave with tools and a plan |

### The Right Mix: Evidence-Based Ratios

Based on adult learning research for skill acquisition:

| Method | Percentage | Rationale |
|--------|------------|-----------|
| **Lecture/Concepts** | 20% | Just enough theory to frame practice |
| **Guided Practice** | 40% | Structured exercises with feedback |
| **Role-Play** | 25% | Simulated real scenarios |
| **Peer Coaching** | 15% | Collaborative problem-solving |

**Critical Success Factor:** Every hour must include active participant doing, not just listening. The brain encodes skills through doing, not hearing.

### Do Participants Leave with Actionable Skills or Just Awareness?

**They leave with:**
- ✅ **Actionable frameworks** - Tools they can apply Monday morning
- ✅ **One polished pitch** - Ready-to-use for a real situation
- ✅ **Completed stakeholder map** - For a current challenge
- ✅ **90-day action plan** - Specific steps to build influence
- ✅ **Accountability buddy** - Support structure

**They do NOT leave with:**
- ❌ Unconscious competence - Skills aren't automatic yet
- ❌ Mastery in complex scenarios - High-stakes situations need more practice
- ❌ Refined personal style - Takes time to integrate and adapt

**Bottom line:** They leave "tool-equipped and plan-ready" not "fully skilled."

## 4. INSTRUCTOR REQUIREMENTS

### Required Expertise Level

| Dimension | Requirement | Why It Matters |
|-----------|-------------|----------------|
| **Subject Matter Depth** | Expert-level | Must answer hard questions and adapt on the fly |
| **Real-World Experience** | 10+ years influencing in organizations | Credibility and stories |
| **Facilitation Skill** | Master-level | Managing energy, time, and 20 adults for 7 hours |
| **Coaching Ability** | Strong | Giving feedback that lands |
| **Business Acumen** | Solid | Understanding shared services context |

### Can Existing Dale Carnegie Instructors Deliver This?

**Short Answer: Maybe, but not automatically.**

| Factor | Assessment | Recommendation |
|--------|------------|----------------|
| **Facilitation Skills** | ✅ Yes - excellent foundation | Most DC instructors are strong facilitators |
| **Influence Content** | ⚠️ Partial - related but different | DC focuses on human relations; this is strategic influence |
| **Business Context** | ⚠️ Variable - depends on instructor | Some have corporate experience; some don't |
| **Complex Role-Play Management** | ⚠️ Needs training | This program has more sophisticated scenarios |

### Instructor Options Ranked

| Option | Pros | Cons | Verdict |
|--------|------|------|---------|
| **Dedicated Specialist** | Deep expertise, tailored delivery | Expensive, limited availability | **Best for pilot and premium offerings** |
| **Trained DC Instructors + Intensive Prep** | Leverages existing assets, scalable | Requires significant upskilling | **Best for scaled rollout** |
| **Hybrid: Specialist + DC Assistant** | Best of both worlds | More complex logistics | **Best for quality at scale** |

### Instructor Training Requirements (if using DC faculty)

**Pre-Requisite:** Complete DC's advanced instructor certification

**Additional Training Needed:**
1. **40-hour Influence Boot Camp certification** - Deep dive into content
2. **Shared services industry immersion** - Understanding the audience
3. **Advanced role-play facilitation** - Managing complex simulations
4. **Mentored co-delivery** - Shadow 2 sessions before leading

**Timeline:** 3-4 months to certify instructors

## FINAL VERDICT: GO with Conditions ✅⚠️

### The One-Day Format CAN Work IF:

1. **Pre-work is mandatory and enforced** - No pre-work = no attendance
2. **Class size is capped at 20** - Larger groups kill interactivity
3. **Instructors are certified specialists** - Not just any facilitator
4. **Follow-up support is built in** - Accountability buddies + 30-day check-in
5. **Expectations are set correctly** - This is foundation-building, not mastery
6. **Content is ruthlessly prioritized** - No "nice to have" modules

### The One-Day Format WILL FAIL IF:

1. ❌ Treated as information download (too much lecture)
2. ❌ Taught by instructors without influence expertise
3. ❌ No follow-through mechanism
4. ❌ Generic business examples instead of shared services context
5. ❌ Class size exceeds 25 participants
6. ❌ Sold as "mastery" instead of "foundation + tools"

## RECOMMENDED IMPLEMENTATION PATH

### Phase 1: Pilot (Months 1-2)
- Run 2 sessions with 12-15 participants each
- Use specialist instructor
- Gather detailed feedback
- Refine content and flow

### Phase 2: Validate (Months 3-4)
- Run 4 sessions with trained DC instructors
- Measure 30/60/90-day outcomes
- Certify additional instructors based on results

### Phase 3: Scale (Month 5+)
- Roll out with certified instructor pool
- Offer quarterly refresher webinars
- Build alumni community
- Consider advanced 2-day version for high-potential cohorts
