# Dale Carnegie Sales SOP
## Business Advancement & B2B Corporate Sales

**Version:** 1.0  
**Created:** February 2026  
**Based on:** Analysis of 13+ sales conversations including WalterFedy, KF Aero, RHI Magnesita, All-Pro.ca

---

## 1. PRE-CALL PREPARATION

### Research Checklist
- [ ] Review company's Dale Carnegie history (past participants, previous courses)
- [ ] Identify mutual connections or referrals
- [ ] Research key decision-makers on LinkedIn
- [ ] Check for recent company news, mergers, or restructuring
- [ ] Review calendar history for previous touchpoints

### Mental Framework
- **Goal:** Listen first, diagnose second, prescribe third
- **Mindset:** You're a trusted advisor, not a vendor
- **Success Metric:** They feel understood, not sold to

---

## 2. OPENING THE CONVERSATION (0-5 minutes)

### The Relationship-First Approach

**Start with connection, not business:**
```
"So good to hear from you."
"I was just checking back... I think you did the course back in [YEAR]."
"Because you were reporting to [NAME] at the time, right?"
```

**Key Techniques:**
1. **Reference shared history:** "We had Stantec sending seven people at a time"
2. **Use mutual connections:** "You know Jordan Underwood, do you?"
3. **Show genuine curiosity about their journey:** "So you're at [NEW COMPANY], how's that?"
4. **Personal warmth:** "Nice to meet you" / "Good to see you"

### Group Calls (BAC/Team Meetings)
```
"Gang, if it is possible to turn on cameras, would really love it. 
Nice to see some faces."

"Appreciate you taking about 90 minutes here for this conversation. 
This probably falls in that second quadrant of important but not urgent. 
So you dedicating time to this makes it important."
```

---

## 3. NEED DISCOVERY (5-20 minutes)

### The "Lift the Hood" Philosophy
> "We think it's important to lift up the hood and look underneath 
> and see what's going on before recommending some kind of solution."

### Discovery Questions by Category

**Business Growth & Strategy:**
- "What's the vision for growing your side of the business?"
- "Are you looking to spread out geographically too?"
- "How's the culture at [COMPANY] compared to [PREVIOUS COMPANY]?"

**Team & Leadership Challenges:**
- "What does the future look like for your team?"
- "How are you handling retention challenges with the younger AMEs?"
- "Are there leadership gaps you're trying to fill?"

**Specific Pain Points (listen for these):**
- Retention challenges with young talent
- Leadership inconsistency from management changes
- Communication breakdowns between departments
- People promoted for technical skills struggling with people management
- "Hunting culture" not developed (waiting for opportunities vs. chasing them)
- Generational differences in soft skills

### Validation & Empathy Techniques

**Use analogies to show understanding:**
```
"I make the distinction between behaviors and structure. 
Think about a big ship - it takes 10 minutes to turn around, 
whether you're motivated or not. The structure's got it."

"I use the analogy of an oscillating fan - you make a little 
progress, but then pressure happens, you go right back."
```

**Acknowledge the systemic nature:**
```
"It's everywhere. We take the smartest person who can do their 
job perfectly and promote them to leader. Three months later 
everybody's complaining. It's not just a [THEIR COMPANY] concern."
```

## 4. SOLUTION POSITIONING (20-35 minutes)

### The Collaborative Approach

**Recap their vision first:**
```
"If I could just recap your vision... I heard you say that you 
want to grow your side of the business and you don't want to rely 
on just a few channel partners. And you've got this young team, 
and they're not as strongly developed socially as your generation 
was. Is that fair?"
```

### Present Options (Not One-Size-Fits-All)

**Traditional Evening Format:**
```
"We still do the evening once a week. What's so great about that 
is you get to practice in between the meetings. That's still a 
real good feature of it."
```

**Lead with Impact (3-Day Intensive):**
```
"We adapted a version called Lead with Impact. It's still the 
Dale Carnegie course, but we bolt on two parts:

1. **Upfront:** One-hour intake session with personality assessment 
   and role clarity discussion with their manager
   
2. **Core:** Three full days of the Dale Carnegie experience 
   (compressed but powerful)
   
3. **Follow-up:** 12 live online reinforcement sessions 
   (they only need 6 for certificate, but many do all 12)
   
Topics include: running effective meetings, goal setting, time 
management, coaching, giving feedback, building momentum."
```

### Pricing & Packaging (Mentioned Casually)

```
"There's usually a price break at three people - it's 10%. 
And if you send seven, you only pay for six. But that shouldn't 
be your main reason to buy. Whatever God and your need, 
we should focus on first."
```

### The "Trusted Advisor" Frame

```
"I just finished a sales course, and the term 'trusted advisor' 
is where it's at. If we could build a group of real good trusted 
advisors that could grow your business for you - have them lead 
people so you don't have to push them."

"I always felt the Dale Carnegie course is just such a match 
for the type of people on your team. It does the whole balance 
of confidence, communication, human skills, leadership, managing 
your head and your attitude."
```

## 5. HANDLING OBJECTIONS

### "We Don't Have Budget for This"

```
"This wasn't part of our budget when we developed our budget 
for this year..."

RESPONSE: "I understand. The way to position it - if you see 
him more as an internal developer, we'd factor in mostly leading 
inside and building people. We've had a lot do it twice, 
especially the Lead with Impact because it's been years since 
their first time through."
```

### "Our People Already Have Technical Training"

```
"He's technically smart, that's all I need to be worrying about..."

RESPONSE: "Time is changing. People are going, I don't think 
that's the right strategy anymore. We're at a point where you're 
correcting something that's broken and trying to reframe it. 
It's a bigger challenge than ever."
```

### "We Can Only Send One or Two People"

```
"I don't know if we would even send two people at a time. 
I think the intent was to send one and no more than two, 
because you want them to be amongst strangers."

RESPONSE: "I always push for teams. I find that's better because 
they get a little bit of collaboration, a little bit of familiar 
faces. At least the people know their business. I'm always a 
proponent of multiples."
```

## 6. ADVANCING THE SALE / NEXT STEPS

### Get Specific on Logistics

```
"Between Hamilton and Kitchener office, how many people would 
be in your group that would do that type of thing?"

"So if we're looking at fall, I just need to circle back..."
```

### Offer Options Based on Their Constraints

```
"I've got one in Kitchener coming up... dates are a little 
changing, but it'll be into March. The Hamilton one is planned 
for March 24th to 26th right now.

I also have an evening class - that's targeted for March 3rd 
for a kickoff. Which one is coming to mind for you first?"
```

### The Personal Connection Close

```
"What's his name? I wonder if I was his instructor... 
Oh, yeah, for sure. He was in an in-house class I did at MT. 
He would remember me too."

(This creates continuity and trust through shared history)
```

## 7. BUSINESS ADVANCEMENT CONVERSATIONS (BAC)

### Group Discovery Format

**Opening:**
```
"Today, the purpose of our meeting is to listen. Everybody's got 
a different view on how things are. We never get to know what 
that is until we talk. So our goal is to ask everybody to speak 
up and contribute even a little bit today."
```

**The Round-Robin Introduction:**
```
"Can we get your name, your role in the organization, and how 
many years you've been in the industry? Let's start with the 
group in the room."
```

**Calculate Collective Experience:**
```
(In background, team member calculates total years)

"Joanna, about how many years do we have represented here?"
"In total, we have an average of 18.7 years for this group."
"318 years total. Wow."

"The reason we make that point - it's not what Joanna and I say 
here today that makes a difference. It's what YOU say, because 
you've got depth and depth of years experience."
```

**Validate Before Prescribing:**
```
"What we're going to do is take back your feedback and get back 
to [LEADER] with our thoughts about how we might be able to 
support you in the future. We think it's important to lift up 
the hood and look underneath before recommending any solution."
```

## 8. KEY PHRASES & LANGUAGE PATTERNS

### Trust-Building Phrases
- "It's great if it's a collaboration between you and I"
- "You've got the vision and everything we do should be on behalf of creating those kinds of people"
- "I trust you - this is your area of specialty"
- "I'm always a proponent of..."
- "I always felt..."

### Discovery Phrases
- "Is that fair?"
- "How would you say?"
- "What does that look like?"
- "Help me understand..."

### Transition Phrases
- "Here's where I'm off or what's new..."
- "My sense was that..."
- "The way I would best describe it is..."

### Empathy Phrases
- "It's all a bit of a blur, isn't it?"
- "Yeah, right on"
- "I can't imagine how you assimilate it all"
- "That's a big challenge"

## 9. CONVERSATION ARCHETYPES

### Type 1: Reconnection (Past Graduates)
- Reference their previous course experience
- Ask about their career journey since
- Connect their past experience to current needs

### Type 2: Referral-Based
- Acknowledge the mutual connection
- Ask about their relationship
- Leverage the trust already established

### Type 3: New Prospect (Cold Discovery)
- Start with industry pain points
- Use analogies to build rapport
- Position as diagnostic, not sales pitch

### Type 4: Group BAC (Business Advancement Conversation)
- Get everyone to introduce themselves
- Calculate collective experience
- Ask for contributions from everyone
- Take notes and promise follow-up

## 10. POST-CALL ACTIONS

### Immediate (Within 1 Hour)
- [ ] Send calendar hold for next meeting
- [ ] Document key pain points discovered
- [ ] Note any specific dates/commitments discussed
- [ ] Research any people/companies mentioned

### Follow-Up (Within 24 Hours)
- [ ] Send personalized follow-up email
- [ ] Include relevant case studies or references
- [ ] Confirm next steps and dates
- [ ] Introduce team members if appropriate

### Long-Term Tracking
- [ ] Log in CRM with relationship notes
- [ ] Set reminder for follow-up if "not now"
- [ ] Track any company changes that might create urgency

## APPENDIX: Sample Discovery Questions by Industry

**Engineering/Consulting Firms:**
- How are you developing the "hunting culture" vs. waiting for opportunities?
- What does the partner track look like for your younger staff?
- How are you handling the transition from technical expert to business developer?

**Manufacturing/Industrial:**
- How do you develop supervisors who were promoted from the floor?
- What systems are in place for consistent leadership across shifts?
- How are you handling the retirement of experienced leaders?

**Construction/Trades:**
- How do your foremen handle the people side vs. just the technical side?
- What does leadership development look like in your organization?
- How do you handle tough conversations with crew members?

**Aviation/Maintenance:**
- How do you retain young AMEs when they have so many options?
- How do you cascade vision from senior leadership to the floor?
- What does accountability look like in your maintenance culture?

## Key Principles to Remember

1. **Listen 70%, Talk 30%** - The best sales conversations are ones where they do most of the talking

2. **Diagnose Before Prescribing** - Never pitch until you truly understand their pain

3. **Be a Trusted Advisor, Not a Vendor** - Your goal is their success, not your sale

4. **Use Their Language** - Mirror the terms and phrases they use to describe their challenges

5. **Create Continuity** - Reference past participants, mutual connections, and shared history

6. **Offer Options** - Present multiple formats (evening vs. intensive) and let them choose

7. **Make It Collaborative** - Frame it as "you and I working together" not "me selling to you"
