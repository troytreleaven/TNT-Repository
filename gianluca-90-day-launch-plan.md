# Dale Carnegie Construction Division
## 90-Day Launch Plan for Gianluca

**Version:** 1.0  
**Target Market:** Small-to-mid-size Ontario construction firms ($3M-$25M revenue)  
**Product:** The Crew Leader Program — 8-week intensive leadership training  
**Price:** $3,000/person (effectively $0 with Canada-Ontario Job Grant)  

---

## Executive Summary

This plan establishes the Dale Carnegie Construction Division in Ontario, targeting the critical leadership gap in small-to-mid-size construction firms. With 72,000 worker shortage in Ontario construction and 20%+ of workforce retiring within 10 years, the need for developing crew leaders has never been greater.

**The Key Leverage Point:** The Canada-Ontario Job Grant (COJG) makes this training essentially free for employers:
- Up to $10,000/trainee for existing employees
- Up to $15,000/trainee + 100% funding for small employers (under 100 employees) hiring unemployed individuals
- Application must be submitted BEFORE training starts

#### First Conversations Structure (Cold/Warm Outreach)

**Discovery Call Framework (30 minutes):**

*Opening (2 min):*
"Thanks for taking the time. I'm Gianluca with Dale Carnegie — we're launching leadership training specifically for Ontario construction. I'm not selling anything today; I'm trying to understand if the problem I'm seeing is real or if I'm imagining it."

*Problem Exploration (10 min):*
- "Walk me through how someone becomes a crew leader at your company."
- "What happens when a great tradesperson gets promoted but doesn't have leadership skills?"
- "How do you currently handle crew conflict or communication breakdowns?"
- "What's your biggest frustration with crew performance?"
- "How much is turnover costing you?"

*Quantify Pain (5 min):*
- "If you had to put a dollar figure on the cost of a crew leader who can't communicate effectively, what would it be?"
- "How many hours per week do you spend dealing with people problems?"

*Solution Preview (8 min):*
- Brief program overview
- COJG funding explanation: *"The province will pay for this training. Your actual out-of-pocket is zero."*
- Pilot program offer

*Close (5 min):*
- "Based on what you've told me, it sounds like [Name] and [Name] would be ideal candidates."
- "Would you be open to joining our pilot cohort? We only have 12 spots."
- "What's your timeline for making a decision?"

#### Construction Culture Communication Guidelines

**DO:**
- Be direct and to the point
- Use concrete examples, not abstract concepts
- Acknowledge their time is valuable
- Show respect for their technical expertise
- Reference job site realities (weather, deadlines, trades)
- Admit when you don't know something

**DON'T:**
- Use corporate buzzwords or "consultant speak"
- Oversell or hype — construction people smell BS
- Waste time with long introductions
- Act like you know their business better than they do
- Ignore the seasonal nature of construction

**Key Phrases That Work:**
- *"Look, I'll be straight with you..."*
- *"Here's the thing..."*
- *"I don't know about your company specifically, but what I'm hearing across the industry is..."*
- *"This isn't for everyone, but..."*
- *"Bottom line: the province pays for it."*

## Decision Trees

### Prospect Interest Level

```
IF prospect says "We need this yesterday":
  → Fast-track to proposal
  → Offer pilot pricing if they commit within 48 hours
  → Schedule COJG application call

IF prospect says "This is interesting, but...":
  → Identify specific objection
  → Provide targeted response
  → Set follow-up in 1 week
  → Send relevant case study/testimonial

IF prospect says "Not right now":
  → Understand timeline
  → Set reminder for follow-up
  → Add to nurture sequence (monthly valuable content)
  → Ask for referral

IF prospect says "No":
  → Understand why (price, timing, don't see need)
  → If don't see need: ask for 5 minutes to understand their situation
  → If price: emphasize COJG
  → If timing: set future follow-up
  → Ask for referral regardless
```

### COJG Application Flow

```
IF company has 50-99 employees AND hiring unemployed:
  → Eligible for 100% funding up to $15,000
  → Must provide job offer letter
  → Must apply BEFORE training starts
  → Timeline: 4-6 weeks for approval

IF company has <50 employees:
  → Eligible for standard funding
  → 5/6 of costs covered (employer pays 1/6)
  → On $3,000 program, employer pays ~$500

IF company has 100+ employees:
  → Eligible for standard funding
  → 2/3 of costs covered (employer pays 1/3)
  → On $3,000 program, employer pays $1,000

IF company has existing employees (not hiring unemployed):
  → Standard funding applies regardless of size
  → Must contribute minimum 1/6
```

### Funding Timing

```
IF application submitted early enough (4-6 weeks before training):
  → Funding approved before training starts
  → Employer may not need to front costs

IF application submitted late:
  → Training may start before approval
  → Employer fronts costs, reimbursed after completion
  → Risk: application denied
  → Recommendation: Submit early, delay start if needed

IF application denied:
  → Understand reason (ineligible trainee, missing docs)
  → Fix issues, reapply if possible
  → Have backup: employer pays full amount
```
