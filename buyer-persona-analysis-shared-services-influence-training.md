# BUYER PERSONA ANALYSIS: INFLUENCE TRAINING FOR SHARED SERVICES PROFESSIONALS

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

**TARGET:** HR, IT, Finance, Accounting, and Marketing professionals in mid-to-large organizations who operate in shared services / consulting models and need to influence without authority.

**VERDICT: CONDITIONAL GO** — This market is viable BUT requires precise targeting and a B2B (not B2C) approach. Individual professionals rarely self-fund; corporate L&D budgets are the primary source.

---

## 1. DETAILED BUYER PERSONAS

### PERSONA 1: "The HR Business Partner" (HRBP)
**Primary Target: HIGH PRIORITY**

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Job Titles** | HR Business Partner, HRBP, People Business Partner, Strategic HR Partner |
| **Seniority** | Mid-level (5-10 years experience), Senior HRBP (10+ years) |
| **Company Size** | 500+ employees, typically 1,000-10,000 |
| **Industry** | Professional services, tech, healthcare, financial services |

**Day-to-Day Pain Points Around Influence:**
- Must influence line managers to implement HR initiatives without having authority over them
- Constantly navigating competing priorities between "the business" and HR headquarters
- Struggling to get managers to take action on retention, engagement, and performance issues
- Working in matrixed environments where dotted-line reporting creates authority gaps
- Acting as "consultants" to business units but lacking formal decision-making power

**Current Solutions Used:**
- Internal leadership development programs (if available)
- SHRM/LinkedIn Learning courses
- Informal mentorship and networking
- Books: "Influencer," "Crucial Conversations," "Getting to Yes"
- Internal HR community of practice meetings

**Quote:** *"I have to get a VP to change their hiring practices, but I can't make them do anything. My success depends entirely on my ability to influence people who outrank me."*

## 2. ORGANIZATIONAL DYNAMICS

### Budget Authority Hierarchy

```
CORPORATE L&D BUDGET (Primary Source)
├── VP/Director of L&D: $500K-$5M+ annual budget
│   └── Decision-maker for enterprise training programs
│
├── Manager/Sr. Manager L&D: $100K-$500K budget
│   └── Decision-maker for department-specific training
│
└── HR Business Partners: $0-$50K discretionary budget
    └── Can recommend, rarely can purchase independently

DEPARTMENT BUDGETS (Secondary Source)
├── Department VP/Director: May have $10K-$100K discretionary
│   └── Can approve function-specific training
│
└── Individual Manager: $1K-$5K per person typically
    └── Can approve for direct reports only

INDIVIDUAL (Rare)
├── Individual Professional: Self-funded (rare in corporate context)
    └── Only when highly motivated career-changers
```

### Typical Training Budgets (2024-2025 Data)

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Average training spend per employee (US) | $874 - $1,254/year |
| Management/leadership training priority | 30% planned budget increases |
| Interpersonal skills training priority | 22% planned budget increases |
| Typical per-course budget | $500 - $3,000 |
| Individual self-funded training (est.) | <5% of professional development market |

### Decision-Making Process for External Training

**For Enterprise/Bulk Purchases:**
1. **Need Identification** (HRBP, Manager, or L&D identifies skill gap)
2. **Requirements Definition** (L&D defines learning objectives)
3. **Vendor Evaluation** (L&D screens providers, often via RFP)
4. **Budget Approval** (VP/Director L&D or CFO for large purchases)
5. **Pilot/Testing** (Small group pilot before rollout)
6. **Procurement** (Legal/Procurement reviews contracts)
7. **Rollout** (Coordinated by L&D with business units)

**For Department-Level Purchases:**
1. Manager identifies need
2. Submits request to Department Head/VP
3. Budget approval (if within department budget)
4. Direct purchase or through L&D coordination

**Timeline:** 3-6 months for enterprise deals; 2-4 weeks for department-level

|-----------|------------|----------|

### Consequences of NOT Solving This Problem

**Individual Level:**
- Career stagnation (can't advance to leadership without influence skills)
- Burnout from constant conflict and resistance
- Limited impact despite high competence
- Perceived as "tactical" not "strategic"

**Organizational Level:**
- Shared services model fails to deliver value (poor adoption of centralized services)
- Projects delayed or fail due to stakeholder resistance
- Cross-functional initiatives stall
- High turnover in shared services roles (frustration with "herding cats")

### Evidence of Active Problem Searching

- LinkedIn Learning has multiple courses on the topic with high enrollment
- SHRM and other professional bodies cover influence skills in conferences

## 4. JOBS-TO-BE-DONE

### The Core Job: "Help me get results through people who don't report to me"

**Functional Jobs:**
- Get stakeholders to commit to action without using formal authority
- Build credibility quickly with new business units/clients
- Navigate matrixed organization politics effectively
- Gain buy-in for unpopular but necessary initiatives
- Resolve conflicts between stakeholders with competing interests

**Emotional Jobs:**
- Feel confident leading without positional power
- Reduce frustration and burnout from constant resistance
- Be seen as a strategic partner, not just a service provider
- Advance career despite not having direct reports

**Social Jobs:**
- Be respected by senior leaders and peers
- Build a reputation as someone who "gets things done"
- Transition from "order-taker" to "trusted advisor"

**Progress They Want to Make:**
1. From "pushing rope" to "pulling stakeholders along"
2. From transactional relationships to strategic partnerships
3. From reactive service delivery to proactive influence
4. From being ignored/overridden to being sought out for input

## 5. OBJECTIONS & BARRIERS

### Why They WOULDN'T Buy This Training

**Financial Objections:**
1. **"My company won't pay for this"** (MOST COMMON)
   - Individual employees rarely self-fund $500-$2,000+ training
   - Requires manager/L&D approval
   - Perception that "soft skills" are less valuable than technical training

2. **"There's no budget for professional development"**
   - True in many organizations post-pandemic/layoffs
   - L&D budgets often cut first during cost reductions

**Time Constraints:**
3. **"I don't have time for training"**
   - Shared services professionals are often overwhelmed
   - Gallup: "Time away from job responsibilities" is the #1 barrier to learning
   - Training seen as taking away from "real work"

4. **"My manager won't give me time off for this"**
   - Manager support is a key obstacle (39% of CHROs cite this)

**Perception Barriers:**
5. **"Influence can't be taught"**
   - Belief that influence is innate personality trait
   - Skepticism about training effectiveness

6. **"This isn't a priority for my role"**
   - Focus on technical certifications (PMP, CPA, SHRM-CP, etc.)
   - Influence skills seen as "nice to have" not "need to have"

**Process Barriers:**
7. **"The procurement process is too complex"**
   - Corporate purchasing requirements
   - Vendor approval processes
   - Legal review of contracts

8. **"I need my manager's approval and they don't see the value"**
   - Managers may not understand the daily influence challenges
   - "Just be more assertive" as inadequate advice

### Price Sensitivity

| Segment | Sensitivity | Notes |
|---------|-------------|-------|
| Enterprise L&D | LOW-MEDIUM | Will pay $10K-$50K+ for bulk licenses |
| Department budgets | MEDIUM | $2K-$10K range, needs clear ROI |
| Individual self-funded | HIGH | Very price-sensitive, <$500 preferred |

## 6. GO/NO-GO RECOMMENDATION

### VERDICT: **CONDITIONAL GO**

This market is viable, but with critical caveats:

**GO IF:**
- You can sell to **L&D/HR decision-makers** (B2B), not individual professionals (B2C)
- You can demonstrate **clear business ROI** (reduced project delays, improved adoption)
- You can **pilot with one department** and expand
- You offer **flexible delivery** (virtual, short sessions, modular) to address time constraints
- You can **integrate with existing leadership development** programs

**PROCEED WITH CAUTION:**
- Individual self-funded market is **extremely limited** (<5% of opportunity)
- Sales cycles are **long** (3-6 months for enterprise)
- **Champions needed** within organizations to navigate procurement

**REVISE STRATEGY IF:**
- You're planning a B2C course priced >$500 — pivot to B2B
- You can't demonstrate tangible outcomes — this market demands ROI proof

## 7. STRATEGIC RECOMMENDATIONS

### Targeting Priority
1. **PRIMARY:** Directors/VPs of L&D in mid-large organizations
2. **SECONDARY:** HRBPs and People Operations leaders who can champion to L&D
3. **TERTIARY:** Department heads with discretionary training budgets

### Messaging Framework
- **Lead with the business problem:** "Shared services failing to deliver value due to poor stakeholder adoption"
- **Quantify the pain:** "Projects delayed by X days due to stakeholder resistance"
- **Offer proof:** Case studies from similar organizations
- **Address time concerns:** "2-hour modules, virtual delivery, immediately applicable"

### Pricing Strategy
- **Enterprise:** $15K-$50K annual license (unlimited users)
- **Department:** $2K-$5K per cohort (20-50 people)
- **Individual:** $299-$499 (not recommended as primary channel)

### Distribution Strategy
- Partner with existing L&D providers
- Direct sales to L&D leadership
- SHRM/ATD conference presence
- LinkedIn content marketing targeting L&D professionals
