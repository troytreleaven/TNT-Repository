# Dale Carnegie Construction Division
## Business Model: Ontario Market Entry

---

## 1. THE OFFER

### What We Sell
**"The Crew Leader Program"** — A practical, 8-week intensive that turns competent tradespeople into profit-driving crew leaders.

Not "leadership training." Not "soft skills." We fix the specific problems that bleed money on job sites.

### Format
- **Duration:** 8 weeks, 3 hours/week (evenings, so crews keep working)
- **Delivery:** In-person at union halls, supplier yards, or contractor offices — wherever foremen already gather
- **Cohort Size:** 12-15 foremen/superintendents max (keeps it tight, peer-to-peer)
- **Structure:** 60% hands-on practice, 40% concept — all scenarios from actual Ontario job sites

### Core Modules (What Actually Matters)
1. **Running the Morning Huddle** — Get 10 guys aligned in 10 minutes, every day
2. **Giving Instructions That Stick** — Stop the "I told him three times" problem
3. **Dealing with the Difficult Guy** — Handle conflict without losing your best worker
4. **Managing Up** — Communicate with the superintendent so problems get solved fast
5. **Crew Retention** — Keep good people from walking to the competitor for $2/hour more

### Positioning
"Most foremen were promoted because they were the best carpenter/plumber/electrician. Nobody taught them how to lead people. This program fills that gap — in language that makes sense on a job site, not in a boardroom."

## 2. TARGET CUSTOMER PROFILE

### Firm Characteristics
| Attribute | Sweet Spot |
|-----------|------------|
| **Revenue** | $3M–$25M annually |
| **Employees** | 15–75 people |
| **Projects** | Commercial, institutional, or multi-unit residential |
| **Structure** | Owner-operated with 2–5 field leaders (foremen/superintendents) |
| **Location** | GTA, Hamilton, Kitchener-Waterloo, Ottawa corridor |

### Pain Profile (The "Night Problems")
These owners wake up at 3am thinking about:
- **Rework costs** — 26% of all rework stems from poor communication (that's real money)
- **Crew productivity** — Labor is 40-60% of costs; small efficiency gains = big profit impact
- **Retention** — Good workers leave for $2/hour more; training cost to replace is $15K–$50K per person
- **Profit margin compression** — Running 7-12% net; one bad job can wipe out a year's profit

### The Decision Maker
**The Owner/GM** — Usually a tradesperson who built the company. Makes buying decisions based on:
1. Trust (who vouched for this)
2. Peer proof (who else they know took it)
3. ROI clarity (what's the payback)

## 4. GO-TO-MARKET

### Phase 1: Gianluca's Network (Months 1–3)
**Goal:** 3 pilot cohorts (36–45 foremen)

**Tactics:**
1. **Warm Introduction Blitz** — Gianluca personally calls 20–30 owner contacts
   - Script: "I'm running a pilot with Dale Carnegie specifically for construction. We're taking 12 foremen for 8 weeks and guaranteeing results. You know anyone with a crew leader who could use this?"

2. **Pilot Pricing** — 50% off first cohort ($1,500/person vs. $3,000 standard) in exchange for:
   - Case study permission
   - Introduction to 2 other owners
   - Video testimonial

3. **Group Pilot Option** — "3 firms, 4 foremen each, same cohort" — creates peer pressure and shared experience

### Phase 2: Referral Engine (Months 4–6)
**Goal:** 50% of new leads from referrals

**Mechanics:**
- Graduates get "Crew Leader Certified" hard hat sticker + certificate for office
- Referral incentive: $500 credit toward next cohort for every firm that signs up
- "Foremen Alumni Network" — monthly 1-hour breakfast meetups (Gianluca hosts, minimal cost, high relationship value)

### Phase 3: Channel Partners (Months 6–12)
**Goal:** Scale through trusted intermediaries

**Partners:**
- **Trade associations** (OGCA, LHBA, local union halls) — offer member discount, they promote
- **Equipment/tool suppliers** — offer joint sessions at their facilities ("Lunch & Lead")
- **Accounting firms serving contractors** — referral fee for client introductions

### Marketing That Works
- **No:** LinkedIn ads, cold calling, corporate brochures
- **Yes:** Gianluca at job site trailers with coffee, union hall lunch presentations, trade show booth at the "have a beer" events (not the suit-and-tie conferences)

## FIRST PRINCIPLES SUMMARY

**What construction owners actually care about:**
1. **Profit margins are thin** — 7-12% is typical; they need every edge
2. **Labor is everything** — 40-60% of costs, hard to find, easy to lose
3. **Communication failures cost real money** — 26% of rework, $17B industry-wide
4. **Trust is the currency** — They buy from people they know or who come recommended
5. **Results must be tangible** — Not "feelings" or "engagement"; hours saved, people retained, mistakes avoided

**How we win:**
- Speak their language (job sites, not boardrooms)
- Deliver measurable outcomes (productivity, retention, profit)
- Leverage Gianluca's network for trust and access
- Keep it simple (one program, done well)
- Guarantee results or don't take the money
